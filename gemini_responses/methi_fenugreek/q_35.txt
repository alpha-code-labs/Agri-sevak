As a Senior Agronomist at Haryana Agricultural University (HAU, Hisar), I can provide you with the scientifically verified information regarding the maturity signs, harvesting time, and cutting process for Methi (Fenugreek) in Haryana. Methi is cultivated for both its tender leaves (as a leafy vegetable) and its seeds.

---

### For Leafy Vegetable Production:

**Maturity Signs:**
*   Plants typically reach a height of 15-20 cm.
*   Leaves are well-developed, tender, succulent, and vibrant green.
*   The plant has not yet started forming flower buds extensively.

**Harvesting Time:**
*   The first cut is generally taken 20-25 days after sowing.
*   Subsequent cuts can be taken at intervals of 15-20 days, depending on the variety, growth rate, and nutrient availability.
*   Up to 3-4 cuts are possible from a single sowing, yielding fresh leaves over an extended period.

**Cutting Process:**
*   Use a sharp knife or sickle to cut the plants.
*   Cut the plants approximately 2-3 cm above the ground level. This ensures that enough stem and basal leaves remain on the plant to facilitate vigorous regrowth for subsequent harvests.
*   Avoid pulling the plants from the roots, as this will prevent regeneration.

---

### For Seed Production:

**Maturity Signs:**
*   The pods on the plant turn from green to yellowish-brown and begin to dry.
*   The seeds inside the pods become hard and fully mature.
*   The lower leaves of the plant start to senesce (turn yellow and dry).
*   The overall plant canopy begins to change color, indicating physiological maturity.

**Harvesting Time:**
*   Harvesting for seeds typically occurs 90-120 days after sowing, depending on the variety and prevailing climatic conditions.
*   The ideal time to harvest is when about 75-80% of the pods have matured and dried.
*   Harvesting too early will result in immature, shriveled seeds, while delaying harvest too much can lead to significant shattering losses of seeds from dry pods.

**Cutting Process:**
*   The entire plant is cut close to the ground level using a sickle.
*   The harvested plants are then gathered into small bundles.
*   These bundles are left in the field or transported to a clean threshing floor for sun-drying for 5-7 days until they are completely dry and brittle.
*   Once thoroughly dried, threshing is performed to separate the seeds from the pods and plant material. This can be done manually by beating the bundles with sticks or by using mechanical threshers.
*   After threshing, the seeds are cleaned to remove chaff and other debris, and then dried further to the recommended moisture content (typically 8-10%) before storage.
