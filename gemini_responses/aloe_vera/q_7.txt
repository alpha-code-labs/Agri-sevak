Regarding the diseases you have mentioned:

*   **Dieback:** This is not known to affect Aloe Vera as a specific disease. While Aloe Vera can exhibit symptoms of tissue death from the tips due to various stresses (e.g., waterlogging, extreme temperatures, nutrient deficiencies, or secondary fungal infections), "Dieback" as a distinct disease is not commonly associated with this crop.
*   **Twig blight:** This is not known to affect Aloe Vera. Aloe Vera is a succulent and does not have twigs.
*   **Tikka disease:** This is not known to affect Aloe Vera. Tikka disease is primarily a leaf spot disease of groundnut (peanut).

If you are observing symptoms such as leaf spots, rotting, or general decline in your Aloe Vera plants, please provide more specific details or photographs.
