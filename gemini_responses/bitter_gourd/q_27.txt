**Climate:**
Bitter Gourd is a warm-season crop that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

*   **Sunlight:** It requires abundant sunshine for good growth and yield.
*   **Frost:** The crop is highly sensitive to frost, which can severely damage the plants.
*   **Suitability for Haryana:** Haryana's climate is well-suited for Bitter Gourd cultivation, particularly during the spring-summer (February-March sowing) and rainy seasons (June-July sowing).

**Soil Type:**
Bitter Gourd can be grown on a wide range of soils, but it performs best in specific types.

*   **Texture:** Well-drained sandy loam to loamy soils are ideal. These soils provide good aeration for root development.
*   **Organic Matter:** Soils rich in organic matter are preferred as they enhance soil fertility and water retention capacity.
*   **pH Level:** A soil pH range of 6.0 to 7.0 (slightly acidic to neutral) is considered optimal for healthy growth.
*   **Avoidance:** Heavy clay soils, which tend to become waterlogged, should be avoided as they are detrimental to root health.

**Drainage Requirement:**
Excellent drainage is crucial for Bitter Gourd cultivation.

*   **Waterlogging:** The crop is highly susceptible to waterlogging. Stagnant water around the roots can lead to root rot, fungal diseases, and ultimately plant death.
*   **Aeration:** Good drainage ensures proper soil aeration, which is essential for healthy root respiration and nutrient uptake.
*   **Management:** In areas prone to heavy rainfall or with naturally poor drainage, cultivation on raised beds or ridges is highly recommended to prevent water accumulation around the root zone.
