It is crucial to adopt an Integrated Disease Management (IDM) approach, combining cultural practices, resistant varieties (if available), and judicious use of chemical controls.

Here's how to control the mentioned conditions:

### 1. Scab (Cucurbit Scab - *Cladosporium cucumerinum*)

Scab is a fungal disease that can affect Snap Melon.

*   **Symptoms:** Characterized by small, water-soaked spots on leaves, stems, and fruits. On fruits, these spots enlarge, become sunken, and develop a dark, velvety growth, often exuding gum.
*   **Control Measures:**
    *   **Cultural Practices:**
        *   Use disease-free seeds.
        *   Practice crop rotation with non-cucurbit crops for at least 2-3 years.
        *   Ensure proper field sanitation by removing and destroying infected plant debris immediately after harvest.
        *   Avoid overhead irrigation to reduce leaf wetness, which favors fungal growth.
        *   Ensure adequate spacing between plants for good air circulation.
    *   **Chemical Control (Apply preventively or at early symptom onset, typically 2-3 sprays at 7-10 day intervals):**
        *   Foliar spray of **Mancozeb** at 2.5 g/L water (or 2.5 kg/acre).
        *   Foliar spray of **Chlorothalonil** at 2 g/L water (or 2 kg/acre).
        *   Foliar spray of **Azoxystrobin** at 0.5 ml/L water (or 200 ml/acre).
        *   Foliar spray of **Tebuconazole** at 1 ml/L water (or 400 ml/acre).

### 2. Canker

"Canker" is a general term for localized necrotic lesions on stems. Bacterial canker (*Clavibacter michiganensis* subsp. *michiganensis*) is **not known to affect Snap Melon**. If you are observing stem lesions or canker-like symptoms on Snap Melon, these are most commonly associated with Gummy Stem Blight, which is addressed under "Gummosis" below.

### 3. Gummosis (Gummy Stem Blight - *Didymella bryoniae* / *Stagonosporopsis cucurbitacearum*)

Gummy Stem Blight is a significant fungal disease in cucurbits, including Snap Melon, and is the primary cause of gummosis.

*   **Symptoms:** This disease causes water-soaked spots on leaves, stems, and fruits. Stem lesions often girdle the stem, leading to wilting, and are characterized by the exudation of amber-colored gum.
*   **Control Measures:**
    *   **Cultural Practices:**
        *   Use disease-free seeds or treat seeds with hot water (55Â°C for 20 minutes) or a recommended fungicide.
        *   Practice strict crop rotation (at least 2-3 years) with non-cucurbit crops.
        *   Remove and destroy infected plant residues immediately after harvest.
        *   Ensure good air circulation within the canopy and avoid excessive humidity.
        *   Avoid wounding plants during cultivation, as wounds can be entry points for the pathogen.
    *   **Chemical Control (Apply preventively or at early symptom onset, typically 2-3 sprays at 7-10 day intervals):**
        *   Foliar spray of **Mancozeb** at 2.5 g/L water (or 2.5 kg/acre).
        *   Foliar spray of **Chlorothalonil** at 2 g/L water (or 2 kg/acre).
        *   Foliar spray of **Azoxystrobin** at 0.5 ml/L water (or 200 ml/acre).
        *   Foliar spray of **Tebuconazole** at 1 ml/L water (or 400 ml/acre).
        *   Foliar spray of **Difenoconazole** at 0.5 ml/L water (or 200 ml/acre).
        *   Foliar spray of **Pyraclostrobin + Metiram** (combination product) at 1.5-2 g/L water (or 600-800 g/acre).

### 4. Lichen

Lichens are symbiotic organisms (a fungus and an alga living together) that grow on the surface of plants, rocks, or other substrates. They are epiphytic and **do not parasitize or cause any harm to Snap Melon plants**. They are not a disease or pest and therefore, no control measures are required or recommended. Their presence often indicates good air quality and a healthy, undisturbed environment.
