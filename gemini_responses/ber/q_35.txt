### Maturity Signs for Ber

The maturity of Ber fruits is primarily judged by visual and tactile cues, which can vary slightly depending on the variety. Look for the following indicators:

*   **Colour Change:** The most prominent sign. Fruits typically change from a dark green to a yellowish-green, then to a golden yellow, and in some varieties, a reddish-brown blush may appear. For example, 'Gola' turns golden yellow, while 'Umran' develops a yellowish-brown colour.
*   **Skin Texture:** The skin becomes smooth and taut, losing its initial dullness. In some varieties, a slight wrinkling may occur when fully ripe, but generally, the skin should be firm.
*   **Flesh Firmness:** The fruit should feel firm to slightly soft when gently pressed, but not mushy. Over-ripe fruits will be very soft.
*   **Taste and Aroma:** Ripe fruits develop their characteristic sweet taste and a pleasant, distinct aroma. The astringency decreases significantly.
*   **Seed Hardness:** The stone (seed) inside the fruit becomes hard and fully developed.

### Harvesting Time for Ber

The harvesting period for Ber in Haryana typically spans from **late January to March**, depending on the variety and local climatic conditions.

*   **Early Varieties (e.g., Gola, Seb):** Usually mature and are ready for harvest from **late January to mid-February**.
*   **Mid to Late Varieties (e.g., Umran, Kaithli):** Generally mature from **mid-February to March**.

Harvesting is usually done in multiple pickings (2-4 times) as fruits do not ripen uniformly on the tree. Pickings are typically spaced 7-10 days apart.

### Cutting Process (Harvesting Method) for Ber

The harvesting of Ber fruits requires care to ensure fruit quality and minimize damage.

*   **Manual Picking:** The most common and recommended method. Fruits should be hand-picked individually when they reach the desired stage of maturity.
*   **Gentle Detachment:** Gently twist or snap the fruit from its pedicel (stalk) at the point of attachment to the branch. Avoid pulling forcefully, which can damage the fruit or the fruiting spur.
*   **Avoid Bruising:** Place the harvested fruits carefully into padded baskets or crates lined with soft material (e.g., cloth, newspaper) to prevent bruising and abrasions. Do not drop fruits from a height.
*   **Harvesting Time of Day:** It is best to harvest fruits during the cooler parts of the day, such as early morning or late afternoon, to reduce field heat and extend shelf life.
*   **Tools for Taller Trees:** For fruits on higher branches, long poles with a net or a hook attachment can be used, but direct hand-picking is always preferred for better quality. Ensure the tools are clean and do not damage the fruit.

By following these guidelines, you can ensure optimal quality and yield from your Ber orchard.
