As a Senior Agronomist at Haryana Agricultural University (HAU, Hisar), I can provide the following scientifically verified advice to improve yield, fruit quality, and storage life for Cowpea (Vegetable) in Haryana.

---

### I. Improving Yield

To maximize cowpea yield, focus on optimal cultural practices and nutrient management:

*   **Variety Selection:** Choose high-yielding, disease-resistant varieties specifically recommended for cultivation in Haryana. Consult HAU's latest recommendations for suitable varieties.
*   **Sowing Time and Spacing:**
    *   **Kharif Season:** Sow from July to August.
    *   **Spring/Summer Season:** Sow from February to March.
    *   **Spacing:** Maintain a row-to-row distance of 45-60 cm and plant-to-plant distance of 10-15 cm.
*   **Nutrient Management:**
    *   **Soil Testing:** Always conduct a soil test to determine specific nutrient deficiencies and tailor fertilizer application.
    *   **Basal Application (per acre):**
        *   Nitrogen (N): 15-20 kg (e.g., 30-45 kg of **Urea**)
        *   Phosphorus (P2O5): 40-60 kg (e.g., 85-130 kg of **Di-ammonium Phosphate (DAP)** or 250-375 kg of **Single Super Phosphate (SSP)**)
        *   Potassium (K2O): 30-40 kg (e.g., 50-65 kg of **Muriate of Potash (MOP)**)
    *   **Micronutrient Application (Foliar Spray):**
        *   **Zinc sulphate:** Spray 0.5% solution (5 g/L of water) at pre-flowering stage if zinc deficiency is observed or suspected.
        *   **Borax:** Spray 0.2% solution (2 g/L of water) at pre-flowering stage if boron deficiency is observed or suspected.
        *   **Ammonium molybdate:** Spray 0.05% solution (0.5 g/L of water) at pre-flowering stage, especially in acidic soils, to enhance nitrogen fixation.
*   **Water Management:** Provide irrigation at critical stages, particularly during flowering and pod development, to ensure adequate moisture. Avoid waterlogging.
*   **Weed Management:** Timely weeding, especially during the initial 30-45 days after sowing, is crucial to reduce competition for nutrients and water.
*   **Plant Growth Regulators (PGRs):**
    *   **Naphthalene acetic acid (NAA):** Apply a foliar spray of 20-40 ppm (20-40 mg/L or 0.02-0.04 g/L of water) at the pre-flowering and early pod initiation stages to improve flower retention and pod set.

### II. Improving Fruit Quality

High-quality cowpea pods are tender, stringless, and have good color.

*   **Variety Selection:** Choose varieties known for producing tender, attractive, and stringless pods with desirable color.
*   **Nutrient Management:** Ensure balanced nutrition, particularly adequate potassium, which plays a vital role in pod development, firmness, and overall quality.
*   **Water Management:** Consistent and adequate moisture throughout the growing period prevents pods from becoming tough, fibrous, or stunted. Avoid drought stress.
*   **Timely Harvesting:** Harvest pods at their optimum maturity stage – when they are young, tender, and fully elongated but before the seeds fully develop and the pods become fibrous. Frequent harvesting (every 2-3 days) encourages continuous pod production and ensures tender pods.
*   **Pest and Disease Management:** Implement integrated pest and disease management strategies to prevent damage to pods, which can compromise their market quality.

### III. Improving Storage Life

Extending the storage life of cowpea pods involves careful post-harvest handling to minimize deterioration.

*   **Harvesting Practices:**
    *   Harvest pods in the early morning or late evening when temperatures are cooler to minimize field heat.
    *   Handle pods gently during harvesting and packing to avoid physical damage (bruising, cuts), which can accelerate spoilage.
*   **Pre-cooling:** Immediately after harvest, rapidly remove field heat by spreading pods in a cool, shaded area or using forced-air cooling if available. This is crucial for extending shelf life.
*   **Cleaning and Sorting:** Carefully sort and remove any damaged, diseased, discolored, or over-mature pods before packing. Only healthy, uniform pods should be stored.
*   **Packaging:** Pack pods in perforated plastic bags (e.g., 100-gauge polyethylene bags with 4-6 ventilation holes) or crates lined with moist newspaper or cloth. This helps maintain high humidity around the pods and reduces moisture loss.
*   **Storage Conditions:**
    *   **Temperature:** Store cowpea pods at a cool temperature of 7-10 °C. Temperatures below 7 °C can cause chilling injury, while higher temperatures accelerate ripening and spoilage.
    *   **Relative Humidity:** Maintain a high relative humidity of 90-95% to prevent desiccation and wilting.
*   **Ethylene Management:** Cowpea pods are sensitive to ethylene. Store them separately from ethylene-producing fruits and vegetables (e.g., ripe tomatoes, bananas, apples) to prevent premature yellowing and senescence.
*   **Post-harvest Treatments:** No specific CIBRC approved chemical treatments are routinely recommended for direct application to fresh cowpea pods by farmers for extending storage life. The primary focus remains on maintaining optimal temperature, humidity, and hygiene.
