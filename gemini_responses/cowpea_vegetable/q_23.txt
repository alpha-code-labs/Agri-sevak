As a Senior Agronomist at Haryana Agricultural University (HAU, Hisar), I recommend the following scientifically verified seed and soil treatment processes for Cowpea (Vegetable) in Haryana:

---

### Seed Treatment Process for Cowpea (Vegetable)

Seed treatment is crucial for protecting young seedlings from seed-borne and soil-borne diseases and early insect pests, as well as enhancing nutrient uptake.

**Order of Application:** Fungicide first, then Insecticide, and finally Bio-fertilizers. Ensure seeds are slightly dry between applications, especially before applying bio-fertilizers.

1.  **Fungicidal Seed Treatment:**
    *   **Purpose:** To protect against seed-borne diseases like anthracnose, damping-off, and early seedling root rots (e.g., caused by *Rhizoctonia* and *Fusarium* species).
    *   **Recommendation:**
        *   Treat seeds with **Carbendazim** 50% WP @ 2 g per kg of seed.
        *   **OR** Treat seeds with **Thiram** 75% WS @ 2.5 g per kg of seed.
        *   *Method:* Mix the fungicide thoroughly with the seeds until they are uniformly coated.

2.  **Insecticidal Seed Treatment:**
    *   **Purpose:** To protect young seedlings from early season sucking pests like aphids, thrips, and shoot fly.
    *   **Recommendation:**
        *   Treat seeds with **Imidacloprid** 70% WS @ 5-7 g per kg of seed.
        *   **OR** Treat seeds with **Thiamethoxam** 30% FS @ 4-5 ml per kg of seed.
        *   *Method:* Apply after fungicidal treatment. Mix the insecticide thoroughly with the seeds.

3.  **Bio-fertilizer Seed Treatment:**
    *   **Purpose:** To enhance nitrogen fixation (critical for legumes) and phosphorus solubilization, leading to better plant growth and yield.
    *   **Recommendation:**
        *   Treat seeds with **Rhizobium** culture (cowpea specific strain) @ 200-250 g for 10-15 kg of seed.
        *   **AND** Treat seeds with **PSB (Phosphate Solubilizing Bacteria)** culture @ 200-250 g for 10-15 kg of seed.
        *   *Method:* Prepare a slurry of the bio-fertilizers with a small amount of water or jaggery solution. Mix this slurry gently with the fungicide and insecticide-treated seeds. Allow the seeds to dry in shade for 30 minutes before sowing.

### Soil Treatment Process for Cowpea (Vegetable)

Soil treatment aims to manage soil-borne diseases, pests, and improve soil health.

1.  **Bio-fungicide Application (for soil-borne diseases):**
    *   **Purpose:** To manage soil-borne diseases like root rot and wilt.
    *   **Recommendation:**
        *   Apply **Trichoderma viride** 1% WP @ 2.5 kg per acre.
        *   **OR** Apply **Pseudomonas fluorescens** 1% WP @ 2.5 kg per acre.
        *   *Method:* Mix the bio-fungicide with 50-100 kg of well-decomposed Farmyard Manure (FYM) or compost. Broadcast this mixture evenly over the field and incorporate it into the soil 7-10 days before sowing.

2.  **Insecticide Application (for soil pests, if known problem):**
    *   **Purpose:** To control soil-borne insect pests such as termites, cutworms, and white grubs.
    *   **Recommendation:**
        *   Apply **Fipronil** 0.3% GR @ 10 kg per acre.
        *   **OR** Apply **Chlorpyrifos** 20% EC @ 2 L per acre.
        *   *Method for Fipronil:* Broadcast evenly and incorporate into the soil before sowing.
        *   *Method for Chlorpyrifos:* Mix with irrigation water or apply as a drench to the soil before sowing or at the time of sowing.

3.  **Nematode Management (if nematodes are a known problem):**
    *   **Purpose:** To reduce the population of plant-parasitic nematodes in the soil.
    *   **Recommendation:**
        *   Incorporate **Neem cake** @ 100-200 kg per acre into the soil 2-3 weeks before sowing.
        *   *Method:* Broadcast the neem cake evenly and mix it thoroughly with the soil. This provides an organic approach to nematode suppression and also improves soil fertility.
