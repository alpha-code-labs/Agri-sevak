### Suitable Climate for Soybean

Soybean (Glycine max) is a warm-season legume crop that thrives in specific climatic conditions:

*   **Temperature:**
    *   **Germination:** Requires a minimum soil temperature of 15-20째C.
    *   **Optimal Growth:** The ideal temperature range for vegetative growth and pod development is 25-30째C.
    *   **Flowering and Pod Filling:** Temperatures above 35째C or below 15째C during flowering and pod filling can adversely affect yield.
    *   **Frost:** Soybean is highly susceptible to frost, especially during flowering and pod development stages.
*   **Rainfall:**
    *   Requires an annual rainfall of 600-1000 mm, well-distributed throughout the growing season.
    *   Critical water requirement stages are flowering and pod development.
    *   In Haryana, it is primarily grown as a Kharif (monsoon) crop, benefiting from monsoon rains. Supplemental irrigation may be required during dry spells.
*   **Sunshine:** Requires adequate sunshine for optimal photosynthesis and yield.

### Suitable Soil Type for Soybean

Soybean can adapt to a range of soil types but performs best in:

*   **Texture:** Well-drained, fertile, medium-textured soils such as sandy loam, loam, or clay loam.
*   **pH:** A soil pH range of 6.0 to 7.5 is considered optimal. It can tolerate slightly acidic to slightly alkaline conditions but prefers neutral to slightly acidic soils.
*   **Organic Matter:** Soils rich in organic matter are highly beneficial as they improve soil structure, water retention, and nutrient availability.
*   **Nutrient Status:** Requires good fertility, particularly adequate phosphorus, potassium, and micronutrients. Being a legume, it fixes atmospheric nitrogen, but an initial starter dose of nitrogen is often beneficial.

### Drainage Requirement for Soybean

Soybean is highly sensitive to waterlogging, making good drainage absolutely critical:

*   **Waterlogging Sensitivity:** Soybean roots are very susceptible to damage from standing water or saturated soil conditions. Even short periods (24-48 hours) of waterlogging can severely impact root respiration, nutrient uptake, nodulation, and overall plant growth, leading to significant yield losses.
*   **Aeration:** The roots require good soil aeration for proper development and for the nitrogen-fixing bacteria (Rhizobium) in the root nodules to function efficiently.
*   **Field Preparation:** Fields should be well-prepared with proper land leveling and provision for excess water drainage, especially in areas prone to heavy monsoon rains. Raised beds or broad-bed furrow (BBF) systems can be beneficial in ensuring adequate drainage.
