### **I. Strategies to Improve Turnip Yield**

To maximize turnip yield, focus on optimal cultural practices and nutrient management:

1.  **Variety Selection:** Choose high-yielding, well-adapted varieties for Haryana conditions. Recommended varieties include Pusa Sweti, Pusa Chandrima, Purple Top White Globe, and Golden Ball.
2.  **Soil Preparation:**
    *   Ensure deep ploughing (20-25 cm) followed by 2-3 harrowing sessions to achieve a fine tilth.
    *   Incorporate 8-10 tonnes of well-decomposed Farm Yard Manure (FYM) or compost per acre during land preparation to improve soil structure and fertility.
    *   Turnips prefer well-drained, sandy loam to loamy soils with a pH range of 6.0-7.0.
3.  **Nutrient Management (Fertilization):**
    *   **Soil Testing:** Always conduct a soil test to determine precise nutrient requirements.
    *   **General Recommendation (without soil test):**
        *   Nitrogen (N): 60-80 kg per acre. Apply half at sowing and the remaining half as a top dressing 30-35 days after sowing.
        *   Phosphorus (P2O5): 40-60 kg per acre. Apply full dose at sowing.
        *   Potassium (K2O): 40-60 kg per acre. Apply full dose at sowing.
    *   **Micronutrients:** Boron deficiency is common and severely affects turnip yield and quality.
        *   Soil application: Apply 4-6 kg of **Borax** per acre at the time of sowing.
        *   Foliar spray: If deficiency symptoms appear (e.g., internal browning, hollow heart), spray **Disodium Octaborate Tetrahydrate** at 1-1.5 g/L of water, 2-3 times at 15-day intervals during root development.
4.  **Water Management (Irrigation):**
    *   Maintain consistent soil moisture, especially during root initiation and development.
    *   Irrigate immediately after sowing for good germination.
    *   Subsequent irrigations should be given at 7-10 day intervals, depending on soil type and weather conditions. Avoid waterlogging.
5.  **Weed Management:**
    *   Weeds compete for nutrients, water, and sunlight, significantly reducing yield.
    *   Perform 1-2 manual weedings within 15-45 days after sowing.
    *   For chemical control, apply pre-emergence herbicide like **Pendimethalin** at 1.0-1.25 kg a.i./acre within 2-3 days of sowing.
6.  **Planting Time and Spacing:**
    *   **Sowing Time:** For plains of Haryana, the optimal sowing time is September to November.
    *   **Spacing:** Sow seeds at a depth of 1-1.5 cm. Maintain a row-to-row distance of 30 cm and plant-to-plant distance of 10-15 cm after thinning.
7.  **Pest and Disease Management:**
    *   **Aphids:** Spray **Imidacloprid** (0.25 ml/L water) or **Thiamethoxam** (0.2 g/L water) upon infestation.
    *   **Diamondback Moth:** Spray **Chlorantraniliprole** (0.3 ml/L water) or **Emamectin Benzoate** (0.2 g/L water) as needed.
    *   **Alternaria Leaf Spot:** Spray **Mancozeb** (2.5 g/L water) or **Chlorothalonil** (2 g/L water) at the appearance of symptoms.
    *   **White Rust:** Spray **Metalaxyl + Mancozeb** (2.5 g/L water) upon disease incidence.

### **II. Strategies to Improve Turnip Root Quality**

Root quality encompasses factors like shape, texture, flavor, and absence of defects.

1.  **Variety Selection:** Choose varieties specifically known for superior root quality, good texture, mild flavor, and uniform shape.
2.  **Nutrient Management:**
    *   **Balanced NPK:** Ensure balanced fertilization. Avoid excessive nitrogen, which can promote leafy growth at the expense of root development and may lead to pithiness or strong flavor.
    *   **Boron:** As mentioned for yield, adequate boron is critical for quality. It prevents internal browning, hollow heart, and corkiness, ensuring smooth, firm roots.
    *   **Calcium:** Calcium is vital for cell wall integrity. Foliar application of **Calcium Nitrate** (5 g/L water) during root development can reduce cracking and improve root firmness.
3.  **Water Management:** Consistent and adequate moisture is crucial. Irregular watering (alternating dry and wet periods) can lead to root cracking, bitterness, and fibrous texture.
4.  **Timely Harvest:** Harvest turnips when they reach optimal size and tenderness. Over-mature roots tend to become woody, fibrous, pithy, and develop a strong, pungent flavor, significantly reducing quality.

### **III. Strategies to Improve Turnip Storage Life**

Proper post-harvest handling and storage conditions are key to extending the shelf life of turnips.

1.  **Variety Selection:** Some turnip varieties inherently possess better storage potential. Consult with HAU experts for such recommendations.
2.  **Careful Harvesting:**
    *   Harvest roots carefully to avoid any physical damage (cuts, bruises, abrasions), which can serve as entry points for pathogens and accelerate spoilage.
    *   Harvest when the soil is relatively dry to minimize soil adherence and potential for rot.
    *   Remove leaves, leaving about 2-3 cm of the petiole attached to the root. This reduces moisture loss and prevents pathogen entry through the cut surface.
3.  **Pre-cooling:** Rapidly remove field heat from harvested turnips by hydro-cooling or forced-air cooling. This slows down respiration, metabolic activity, and moisture loss, significantly extending shelf life.
4.  **Optimal Storage Conditions:**
    *   **Temperature:** Store turnips at a low temperature, ideally 0-1Â°C. This is the most critical factor for extending storage life.
    *   **Relative Humidity:** Maintain a high relative humidity of 90-95% to prevent desiccation, shriveling, and weight loss.
    *   **Ventilation:** Ensure good air circulation within the storage area to prevent the accumulation of ethylene and other gases, and to maintain uniform temperature and humidity.
5.  **Post-Harvest Disease Management:**
    *   Thoroughly inspect roots before storage and discard any damaged, diseased, or bruised turnips.
    *   Maintain strict sanitation in storage facilities to prevent the spread of fungal and bacterial rots.
6.  **Ethylene Management:** Store turnips away from fruits that produce high levels of ethylene (e.g., apples, bananas), as ethylene can accelerate senescence and spoilage in turnips.
