As a Senior Agronomist at Haryana Agricultural University (HAU, Hisar), I understand the challenges weeds pose to Drum Stick (Moringa oleifera) cultivation. Effective weed management is crucial, especially during the establishment phase, to ensure healthy growth and good yields.

Weeds compete with Drum Stick plants for water, nutrients, sunlight, and space, leading to significant yield losses if not controlled. An integrated approach combining cultural, mechanical, and chemical methods is recommended.

**Integrated Weed Management in Drum Stick:**

1.  **Cultural and Mechanical Methods:**
    *   **Manual Weeding:** Hand weeding is highly effective, especially for young plants and for removing specific problematic weeds like Amarbail. It should be done regularly, particularly during the initial 3-4 months after planting.
    *   **Hoeing:** Shallow hoeing can effectively control young weeds and improve soil aeration.
    *   **Mulching:** Applying organic mulch (e.g., straw, dry leaves, crop residues) around the base of Drum Stick plants can suppress weed growth, conserve soil moisture, and regulate soil temperature.
    *   **Intercropping:** In wider row spacing, suitable short-duration, shallow-rooted crops can be grown between Drum Stick rows to suppress weeds and provide additional income.

2.  **Chemical Weed Control (Herbicides):**
    Herbicides can be an effective tool, but their application must be precise and careful to avoid damage to the Drum Stick plants. Always use CIBRC approved chemicals and follow label instructions strictly.

Here's how to control the specific weeds you mentioned:

*   **Broadleaf and Narrowleaf Weeds (General Control):**
    These categories include a wide range of common weeds.

    *   **Pre-emergence Herbicides (Applied before weed emergence):**
        *   **Pendimethalin 30% EC:** Apply at **3.3 - 5.0 Litres per acre** in 200-300 Litres of water. This should be applied within 3 days of planting or transplanting, or as a directed spray in established plantations before weed emergence. It controls many annual grasses and broadleaf weeds.
        *   **Oxyfluorfen 23.5% EC:** Apply at **1.0 Litre per acre** in 200-300 Litres of water as a pre-emergence application. It provides good control of a wide range of annual broadleaf and grassy weeds.

    *   **Post-emergence Herbicides (Applied after weed emergence):**
        *   **Glyphosate 41% SL:** This is a non-selective herbicide, highly effective against most broadleaf and narrowleaf weeds. Apply at **2.5 - 5.0 Litres per acre** in 200-300 Litres of water as a directed spray, ensuring no contact with the Drum Stick foliage or green stems. Use a protective shield or hood to prevent drift.
        *   **Paraquat dichloride 24% SL:** This is a contact herbicide, effective for quick knockdown of emerged weeds. Apply at **2.0 - 4.0 Litres per acre** in 200-300 Litres of water as a directed spray, strictly avoiding contact with Drum Stick plants.

*   **Amarbail (Cuscuta spp.):**
    Amarbail is a parasitic weed that can severely damage Drum Stick.
    *   **Manual Removal:** This is the most effective method. Regularly inspect plants and manually remove any Amarbail strands as soon as they appear, before they establish firmly. Remove the affected part of the Drum Stick plant if necessary.
    *   **Prevention:** Ensure seeds are not introduced through contaminated planting material or irrigation water.
    *   **Pre-emergence:** Application of **Pendimethalin 30% EC** (as mentioned above) can help reduce the germination of Amarbail seeds in the soil.

*   **Bathu (Chenopodium album):**
    Bathu is a common broadleaf weed.
    *   It can be effectively controlled by manual weeding, hoeing, and the general broadleaf herbicides mentioned above, such as **Glyphosate 41% SL** (directed spray) or **2,4-D Sodium Salt 80% WP** (see Congress Grass below).

*   **Congress Grass (Parthenium hysterophorus):**
    Parthenium is a highly invasive broadleaf weed.
    *   **Manual Removal:** Remove Parthenium plants before flowering to prevent seed dispersal. Wear gloves and protective clothing as it can cause allergic reactions.
    *   **Chemical Control:**
        *   **Glyphosate 41% SL:** Apply as a directed spray at **2.5 - 5.0 Litres per acre** in 200-300 Litres of water, ensuring no contact with Drum Stick plants.
        *   **2,4-D Sodium Salt 80% WP:** Apply at **625 - 1250 grams per acre** in 200-300 Litres of water as a directed spray for broadleaf weeds like Parthenium. Avoid contact with Drum Stick foliage.

*   **Orobanche (Broomrape spp.):**
    This is not known to affect Drum Stick.

*   **Phalaris minor (Little seed canary grass):**
    Phalaris minor is a common narrowleaf (grassy) weed.
    *   It can be controlled by manual weeding, hoeing, and the general narrowleaf/grass herbicides mentioned above, such as **Pendimethalin 30% EC** (pre-emergence) or **Glyphosate 41% SL** (post-emergence, directed spray).
    *   **Selective Grass Control:** If grassy weeds are a predominant problem and broadleaf weeds are not, you may consider:
        *   **Quizalofop-ethyl 5% EC:** Apply at **1.0 - 2.0 Litres per acre** in 200-300 Litres of water for selective control of grassy weeds. While generally safe for broadleaf crops, always exercise caution and ensure no direct spray on Drum Stick foliage.

**Important Considerations and Precautions:**

*   **Timing:** Apply pre-emergence herbicides to a moist soil surface for best results. Post-emergence herbicides are most effective on young, actively growing weeds.
*   **Directed Spray:** When using non-selective herbicides like **Glyphosate** or **Paraquat dichloride**, always use a spray shield or hood to prevent herbicide drift onto the Drum Stick plants, especially young ones.
*   **Water Volume:** Ensure adequate water volume (200-300 Litres per acre) for uniform coverage.
*   **Safety:** Always wear appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) such as gloves, masks, and protective clothing while handling and applying herbicides.
*   **Calibration:** Calibrate your sprayer regularly to ensure correct dosage application.
*   **Consult Experts:** For specific situations or if you are unsure, always consult with your local agricultural extension officer or HAU experts.

By adopting these scientifically verified practices, you can effectively manage weeds in your Drum Stick cultivation and ensure a healthy, productive crop.
