As a Senior Agronomist at Haryana Agricultural University (HAU, Hisar), I understand the challenges posed by sucking pests in Drum Stick (Moringa oleifera) cultivation. Aphids, Leafhoppers, Whiteflies, Thrips, and Psylla can significantly reduce yield and plant vigour. Effective control requires an integrated approach.

Here's a scientifically verified strategy to manage these pests:

**Integrated Pest Management (IPM) Approach:**

1.  **Regular Monitoring:**
    *   Inspect your Drum Stick plants regularly, especially the undersides of leaves and tender shoots, for the presence of pests. Early detection is key to effective control.
    *   Look for symptoms like curling leaves, stunted growth, sticky honeydew, and sooty mould.

2.  **Cultural Practices:**
    *   **Weed Management:** Keep the field and surrounding areas free of weeds, as they can act as alternate hosts for these pests.
    *   **Proper Nutrition:** Ensure balanced fertilization. Over-fertilization with nitrogen can lead to lush, tender growth that attracts sucking pests.
    *   **Pruning:** Remove and destroy heavily infested leaves or shoots to reduce pest populations.
    *   **Water Management:** Avoid water stress, as stressed plants are more susceptible to pest attacks.
    *   **Yellow Sticky Traps:** Install yellow sticky traps (10-15 traps per acre) to monitor and mass-trap adult whiteflies and leafhoppers.

3.  **Biological Control:**
    *   **Conserve Natural Enemies:** Encourage beneficial insects like ladybird beetles, lacewings, and parasitic wasps, which prey on aphids, whiteflies, and other sucking pests. Avoid broad-spectrum insecticides that harm these beneficials.
    *   **Neem-based formulations:** Spraying neem oil (Azadirachtin 1500 ppm or 3000 ppm) at a dose of 5 ml/L of water can act as a repellent, antifeedant, and growth disruptor, especially in early stages of infestation.

**Chemical Control (If infestation is severe and other methods are insufficient):**

When chemical intervention becomes necessary, always use CIBRC approved insecticides and follow the recommended dosages. Rotate insecticides with different modes of action to prevent the development of resistance. Ensure thorough coverage, especially on the undersides of leaves.

Here are some CIBRC approved technical chemical options effective against a range of sucking pests:

*   **For Aphids, Leafhoppers, Whiteflies, Thrips, and Psylla:**
    *   **Imidacloprid 17.8% SL:** 0.5 - 1 ml per litre of water.
    *   **Thiamethoxam 25% WG:** 0.2 - 0.4 g per litre of water.
    *   **Acetamiprid 20% SP:** 0.2 - 0.4 g per litre of water.
    *   **Dinotefuran 20% SG:** 0.3 - 0.5 g per litre of water.

*   **Specifically for Whiteflies and Thrips (also effective on some other sucking pests):**
    *   **Diafenthiuron 50% WP:** 1.25 - 1.5 g per litre of water.
    *   **Buprofezin 25% SC:** 1.25 - 1.5 ml per litre of water (especially effective against nymphs of whiteflies and hoppers).

**Important Considerations:**

*   **Safety First:** Always wear protective clothing (gloves, mask, goggles) while handling and spraying chemicals.
*   **Pre-Harvest Interval (PHI):** Observe the recommended pre-harvest interval for each chemical to ensure the produce is safe for consumption.
*   **Spray Timing:** Spray during cooler parts of the day (early morning or late evening) to minimize drift and maximize efficacy.
*   **Water Quality:** Use clean water for spraying.

By adopting these integrated strategies, you can effectively manage sucking pests in your Drum Stick crop and ensure a healthy yield. For any specific concerns or severe outbreaks, please consult your local agricultural extension officer or HAU experts.
