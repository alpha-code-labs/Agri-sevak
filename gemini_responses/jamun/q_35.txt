### Maturity Signs for Jamun

The maturity of Jamun fruits is primarily determined by visual and tactile cues:

*   **Colour Change:** The most prominent sign is the change in fruit colour. Immature fruits are green. As they mature, they turn pinkish-red, then progressively deepen to a dark purple or almost black colour. Fully mature fruits will have a uniform deep purple to black skin.
*   **Size:** Fruits attain their full varietal size. While size can vary between varieties, mature fruits will stop growing and reach their characteristic dimensions.
*   **Texture:** The fruit pulp softens slightly but remains firm. Overripe fruits become very soft and mushy.
*   **Taste:** The characteristic sweet-sour taste develops fully. Immature fruits are highly astringent.
*   **Ease of Detachment:** Mature fruits detach relatively easily from the stalk with a gentle pull.

### Harvesting Time for Jamun

In Haryana, Jamun fruits are typically harvested during the **monsoon season**, which generally spans from **May to July**. The peak harvesting period is usually in **June and July**, depending on the specific variety and local climatic conditions. Since fruits ripen unevenly on the tree, multiple pickings are often required over a period of 2-3 weeks.

### Cutting Process (Harvesting Method) for Jamun

The harvesting of Jamun requires careful handling to prevent damage to the fruit, which is delicate and prone to bruising.

*   **Hand Picking:** The most recommended method is hand-picking. This allows for selective harvesting of only fully mature fruits and minimizes damage.
    *   Gently grasp the fruit or a small cluster of fruits and twist or pull them off the stalk.
    *   Avoid pulling forcefully, which can damage the fruit or the branch.
*   **Using Harvesting Tools:** For fruits on higher branches, specialized tools can be used:
    *   **Fruit Pickers with Nets:** These consist of a long pole with a basket or net at the end, often equipped with a cutting or detaching mechanism. This allows fruits to fall directly into the net, preventing bruising from impact with the ground.
    *   **Long Poles with Hooks/Blades:** These can be used to gently detach fruit clusters, but care must be taken to ensure fruits do not fall from a height onto hard surfaces.
*   **Avoid Shaking:** Do not shake the tree to dislodge fruits, as this causes significant bruising, reduces fruit quality, and can damage the tree branches.
*   **Collection:** Harvested fruits should be collected in shallow baskets or crates lined with soft material (like cloth or leaves) to prevent bruising during transport. Avoid piling fruits too high.
*   **Multiple Pickings:** As Jamun fruits ripen asynchronously, it is essential to conduct multiple rounds of harvesting (every 3-5 days) to ensure only optimally mature fruits are picked.
