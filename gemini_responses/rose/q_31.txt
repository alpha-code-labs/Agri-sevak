Let's address each of the conditions you've mentioned:

*   **Fruit bursting in Rose:** This is not known to affect Rose as a significant problematic condition requiring specific control measures. Rose hips (the fruit of the rose) are generally not prone to bursting in the way that some commercial fruits like tomatoes or cherries are.
*   **Hardness in fruit (Rose hips):** This is not known to affect Rose as a problematic condition. Rose hips naturally harden as they mature and dry, which is a normal part of their development.

*   **Leaf burning/melting in Rose:** This is a common issue in roses, often referred to as leaf scorch or sunburn, and can lead to severe tissue damage that might appear as "melting" in extreme cases. It is primarily caused by environmental stress or improper cultural practices.

Here are the control measures for leaf burning/melting in roses:

1.  **Proper Irrigation Management:**
    *   **Consistent Watering:** Ensure a consistent water supply, especially during hot and dry periods. Irregular watering (long dry spells followed by heavy irrigation) can stress plants and make them more susceptible to scorch.
    *   **Timing:** Water early in the morning. Avoid overhead irrigation during the hottest part of the day, as water droplets on leaves can act as lenses, intensifying sunlight and causing burns.
    *   **Adequate Depth:** Water deeply to encourage root development, rather than frequent shallow watering.

2.  **Protection from Intense Sun and Heat:**
    *   **Shade Netting:** In areas with extremely high summer temperatures and intense solar radiation (common in Haryana), consider providing temporary shade using 30-50% shade netting during the peak summer months (April to June).
    *   **Mulching:** Apply a 5-10 cm layer of organic mulch (e.g., straw, wood chips, dried leaves) around the base of the plants. This helps to conserve soil moisture, regulate soil temperature, and reduce heat stress on roots.

3.  **Correct Chemical Application:**
    *   **Follow Label Instructions:** Always adhere strictly to the recommended dosage and application instructions for all pesticides, fungicides, and foliar fertilizers.
    *   **Avoid High Temperatures:** Do not spray chemicals during the hottest part of the day or when temperatures are excessively high (above 30Â°C), as this can lead to phytotoxicity (chemical burn) on leaves.
    *   **Compatibility:** Ensure compatibility when mixing multiple products to avoid adverse reactions.

4.  **Balanced Nutrition:**
    *   **Soil Testing:** Conduct regular soil tests to determine nutrient deficiencies or excesses.
    *   **Balanced Fertilization:** Provide a balanced fertilizer regimen based on soil test results. Over-fertilization, especially with nitrogen, can lead to lush, tender growth that is more susceptible to scorch. Excess salts in the soil can also cause leaf burn.
    *   **Micronutrients:** Ensure adequate availability of micronutrients, as deficiencies can weaken plants.

5.  **Wind Protection:**
    *   **Windbreaks:** Strong, hot, dry winds can desiccate leaves and contribute to burning. Consider planting windbreaks or using temporary barriers to protect rose beds from prevailing winds.

By implementing these cultural practices, you can significantly reduce the incidence of leaf burning and melting in your rose plants, ensuring healthier growth and better flower production.
