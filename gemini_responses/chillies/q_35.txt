---

### Maturity Signs for Chillies

The maturity signs for chillies depend on whether they are intended for green consumption or for drying/red chilli powder.

*   **For Green Chillies (Vegetable Purpose):**
    *   The fruits attain their full size characteristic of the variety.
    *   They exhibit a firm texture and a bright, uniform green colour.
    *   The skin appears glossy.
    *   Harvesting at this stage ensures pungency and crispness suitable for fresh consumption.

*   **For Red Chillies (Drying or Powder Purpose):**
    *   The fruits are fully ripened and have developed a uniform deep red colour.
    *   They may show a slight wrinkling if left on the plant for extended ripening, indicating a reduction in moisture content.
    *   The stalk (pedicel) may start to dry slightly.
    *   Harvesting at this stage maximizes capsaicin content (pungency) and colour development.

### Harvesting Time for Chillies

The timing and frequency of harvesting are crucial for optimal yield and quality.

*   **Initiation:** Harvesting typically begins 60-90 days after transplanting, depending on the variety and growing conditions.
*   **Frequency:**
    *   **Green Chillies:** Harvesting should be done every 5-7 days, or up to 10 days, depending on the rate of fruit development and market demand. Regular harvesting encourages further flowering and fruiting.
    *   **Red Chillies:** Harvesting can be less frequent, typically every 10-15 days, allowing fruits to fully ripen on the plant.
*   **Time of Day:** It is best to harvest chillies in the early morning after the dew has dried, or in the late afternoon. Harvesting during the hottest part of the day should be avoided as it can lead to rapid wilting and reduced post-harvest quality.

### Cutting Process for Chillies

The method of harvesting significantly impacts the plant's health and the quality of the harvested fruit.

*   **Method:** Hand picking is the most common and recommended method for chillies. This allows for selective harvesting of mature fruits without damaging the plant.
*   **Technique:**
    *   Gently snap or cut the chilli fruit along with a short portion of its stalk (pedicel) attached. This helps in better storage life and prevents the entry of pathogens through the fruit scar.
    *   Avoid pulling or tearing the fruits directly from the plant, as this can damage the branches, flowers, and developing fruits, thereby reducing future yields.
    *   For varieties with tough stalks or for larger operations, small, sharp secateurs or a knife can be used, ensuring a clean cut.
    *   Collected chillies should be placed carefully into clean baskets or crates, avoiding overfilling to prevent crushing and bruising.
    *   Regular harvesting also helps in maintaining plant vigour and promoting continuous fruiting.
