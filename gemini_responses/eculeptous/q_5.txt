### Canker in Eucalyptus

Canker in Eucalyptus can be caused by various fungal pathogens, including species of *Botryosphaeria* and *Cryphonectria*. Symptoms include sunken, discolored lesions on stems and branches, often accompanied by gum exudation, leading to girdling and dieback of affected parts.

**Control Measures:**
*   **Cultural:**
    *   Prune infected branches well below the visible canker, ensuring clean cuts.
    *   Disinfect pruning tools thoroughly between cuts to prevent disease spread.
    *   Apply a protective fungicidal paste (e.g., Bordeaux paste) to large wounds immediately after pruning.
*   **Chemical:** Apply systemic fungicides as a foliar spray or drench around the base of affected trees.
    *   **Carbendazim** 1 g/L of water (or 200 g/acre in 200 L water for foliar spray/drench).
    *   **Propiconazole** 0.5 ml/L of water (or 100 ml/acre in 200 L water for foliar spray/drench).
    *   **Thiophanate-methyl** 1 g/L of water (or 200 g/acre in 200 L water for foliar spray/drench).

### Gummosis in Eucalyptus

Gummosis is the exudation of gum from the bark, which is a physiological response of the tree rather than a disease itself. It is often a symptom of underlying stress factors such as drought, waterlogging, nutrient deficiencies, insect borers (e.g., stem borers), or fungal infections (e.g., *Phytophthora* species causing root rot, or *Botryosphaeria* species).

**Control Measures:**
*   **Identify and address the underlying cause:**
    *   **Cultural:** Ensure proper irrigation (avoiding both drought and waterlogging), improve soil drainage, and provide balanced nutrition.
    *   **Insect Management:** If insect borers are suspected, consult an entomologist for specific identification and appropriate insecticide recommendations.
    *   **Fungal Management:** If fungal pathogens (e.g., *Phytophthora* causing root rot) are identified as the primary cause, apply appropriate fungicides.
        *   **Metalaxyl + Mancozeb** (combination product) 2.5 g/L of water (or 500 g/acre in 200 L water as soil drench or foliar spray).
        *   **Propiconazole** 0.5 ml/L of water (or 100 ml/acre in 200 L water as soil drench or foliar spray).

### Lichen in Eucalyptus

Lichen are symbiotic organisms composed of a fungus and an alga or cyanobacterium. They grow superficially on the bark of trees, including Eucalyptus. Lichens are generally **harmless** to the tree; they do not parasitize the tree, extract nutrients, or cause disease. Their presence often indicates good air quality.

**Control Measures:**
*   **No control measures are typically required** as lichens do not harm the tree.
*   If their presence is aesthetically undesirable, improving tree vigor through proper nutrition and watering can sometimes reduce their growth, but direct chemical control is not recommended for tree health.
