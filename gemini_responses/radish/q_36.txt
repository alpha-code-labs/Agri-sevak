### I. Improving Radish Yield

To maximize radish yield, focus on optimal cultural practices and nutrient management:

*   **Variety Selection:** Choose high-yielding varieties suitable for Haryana's climate. Recommended varieties include Pusa Chetki, Pusa Rashmi, Japanese White, and Rapid Red.
*   **Soil Preparation:** Radish thrives in well-drained, loose, sandy loam soil with a pH of 6.0-7.0. Deep ploughing (20-25 cm) followed by harrowing to achieve a fine tilth is crucial for proper root development.
*   **Nutrient Management:**
    *   **Organic Manure:** Incorporate 15-20 tonnes/acre of well-decomposed Farm Yard Manure (FYM) or compost during land preparation.
    *   **Inorganic Fertilizers:**
        *   Nitrogen (N): Apply 40-50 kg/acre. Apply half at sowing and the remaining half as a top dressing 15-20 days after sowing.
        *   Phosphorus (P2O5): Apply 20-30 kg/acre as a basal dose.
        *   Potassium (K2O): Apply 40-50 kg/acre as a basal dose.
    *   **Micronutrients:** If soil tests indicate deficiency, apply micronutrients. For instance, Boron deficiency can lead to internal browning.
        *   **Boron:** Apply 1-2 kg/acre of **Borax** (Sodium tetraborate decahydrate) or **Disodium Octaborate Tetrahydrate** as a soil application or 0.2% foliar spray if deficiency symptoms appear.
*   **Irrigation:** Radish requires consistent moisture, especially during root development. Provide light and frequent irrigations. Avoid waterlogging and severe moisture stress, as both can negatively impact yield and quality.
*   **Weed Management:** Weeds compete for nutrients and moisture, reducing yield. Perform manual weeding or inter-cultivation 15-20 days after sowing and again 10-15 days later.
*   **Planting Density:** Maintain optimal spacing for proper root development. A spacing of 30 cm between rows and 7-10 cm between plants within a row is generally recommended.

### II. Improving Radish Root Quality

High-quality radish roots are firm, well-shaped, free from cracks, and have desirable pungency and texture.

*   **Variety Selection:** Select varieties known for good root shape, texture, and pungency.
*   **Soil Type:** Ensure loose, friable soil to allow for uniform root expansion and prevent malformation.
*   **Nutrient Management:**
    *   **Balanced NPK:** Adequate and balanced nutrition is vital. Potassium plays a significant role in root development and quality.
    *   **Boron:** Deficiency can cause internal browning, cracking, and hollow heart. Ensure sufficient **Boron** (as mentioned above) is available.
    *   **Calcium:** Adequate **Calcium** can improve cell wall integrity, leading to firmer roots and reduced physiological disorders.
*   **Irrigation:** Consistent and uniform irrigation is critical. Irregular watering (alternating between dry and wet conditions) can lead to root cracking, pithiness, and bitterness.
*   **Harvesting Time:** Harvest radish roots at their optimum maturity, when they reach marketable size but before they become woody, fibrous, or pithy. Over-mature roots rapidly decline in quality.

### III. Improving Radish Storage Life

Extending the storage life of radish involves careful post-harvest handling and appropriate storage conditions.

*   **Harvesting:**
    *   Harvest early in the morning when temperatures are cooler.
    *   Handle roots gently to avoid physical damage (bruises, cuts), which can be entry points for pathogens.
    *   Remove the leaves immediately after harvest, leaving about 2-3 cm of the petiole. Leaves cause rapid moisture loss and accelerate spoilage.
*   **Pre-cooling:** Rapidly remove field heat immediately after harvest. Hydro-cooling (dunking in cold water) or forced-air cooling to 0-5°C is highly effective in slowing down respiration and metabolic processes.
*   **Cleaning:** Gently wash roots to remove soil, but ensure they are thoroughly dried before packaging to prevent fungal growth.
*   **Packaging:**
    *   Pack radishes in perforated plastic bags or crates lined with moist paper or plastic film. This helps maintain high humidity (90-95%) and prevents desiccation.
*   **Storage Conditions:**
    *   Store radishes at a temperature of 0-2°C with 90-95% relative humidity. Under these conditions, radishes can be stored for 2-4 weeks.
*   **Post-harvest Treatments:**
    *   **Calcium Chloride:** Dipping radish roots in a 1-2% solution of **Calcium Chloride** for 5-10 minutes after washing can significantly improve root firmness and reduce physiological disorders, thereby extending their shelf life. Ensure roots are air-dried before packaging.
