As a Senior Agronomist at Haryana Agricultural University (HAU, Hisar), I understand your concern regarding viral diseases in Rajma. It is crucial to understand that there are no direct chemical treatments (viricides) to cure plants once they are infected with a virus. Therefore, the primary focus of viral disease management is on prevention, using resistant varieties, and controlling the insect vectors that transmit these viruses.

Here's how to manage the mentioned viral diseases in Rajma:

### General Principles for Viral Disease Management in Rajma:

1.  **Resistant Varieties:** Always choose Rajma varieties known to be resistant or tolerant to common viral diseases prevalent in Haryana. Consult HAU for recommended varieties.
2.  **Virus-Free Seeds:** Use certified, virus-free seeds. Many viruses, like Bean Common Mosaic Virus (BCMV), are seed-borne.
3.  **Vector Control:** Most plant viruses are transmitted by insect vectors such as whiteflies, aphids, and thrips. Effective management of these vectors is critical.
4.  **Roguing:** Regularly inspect your field and promptly remove and destroy (bury or burn) any plants showing symptoms of viral infection. This prevents the spread of the virus to healthy plants.
5.  **Weed Management:** Keep your fields and surrounding areas free of weeds, as many weeds can act as alternate hosts for both viruses and their insect vectors.
6.  **Crop Rotation:** Practice crop rotation with non-host crops to break the disease cycle.
7.  **Sanitation:** Clean farm tools and equipment to prevent mechanical transmission of viruses.

### Specific Viral Diseases and Their Control in Rajma:

1.  **Leaf Curl:**
    *   **Affects Rajma:** Yes, various geminiviruses can cause leaf curl symptoms in Rajma, primarily transmitted by whiteflies.
    *   **Control Measures:**
        *   Plant resistant varieties.
        *   Implement strict whitefly control measures from the early stages of crop growth.
        *   **Chemical Control for Whiteflies:**
            *   **Imidacloprid** 17.8% SL: 125-250 ml per acre (or 0.3-0.5 ml/L of water).
            *   **Thiamethoxam** 25% WG: 50-100 g per acre (or 0.2-0.4 g/L of water).
            *   **Acetamiprid** 20% SP: 50-100 g per acre (or 0.2-0.4 g/L of water).
        *   Regular roguing of infected plants.

2.  **Mosaic (e.g., Bean Common Mosaic Virus - BCMV, Bean Yellow Mosaic Virus - BYMV, Cucumber Mosaic Virus - CMV):**
    *   **Affects Rajma:** Yes, mosaic diseases are very common and significant in Rajma. BCMV is seed-borne and aphid-borne, while BYMV and CMV are primarily aphid-borne.
    *   **Control Measures:**
        *   Use certified virus-free seeds, especially important for BCMV.
        *   Plant resistant varieties.
        *   Implement effective aphid control measures.
        *   **Chemical Control for Aphids:**
            *   **Imidacloprid** 17.8% SL: 125-250 ml per acre (or 0.3-0.5 ml/L of water).
            *   **Thiamethoxam** 25% WG: 50-100 g per acre (or 0.2-0.4 g/L of water).
            *   **Acetamiprid** 20% SP: 50-100 g per acre (or 0.2-0.4 g/L of water).
            *   **Dimethoate** 30% EC: 330-660 ml per acre (or 1.0-2.0 ml/L of water).
        *   Prompt roguing of infected plants.

3.  **Yellow Vein Clearing:**
    *   **Affects Rajma:** Yes, symptoms like yellow vein clearing are often associated with geminiviruses (e.g., Mungbean Yellow Mosaic Virus) which are transmitted by whiteflies.
    *   **Control Measures:**
        *   Similar to Leaf Curl, focus on resistant varieties and stringent whitefly control.
        *   **Chemical Control for Whiteflies:** (Refer to Leaf Curl section for specific chemicals and dosages).
        *   Regular roguing of infected plants.

4.  **Stripe:**
    *   **Affects Rajma:** This is not known to affect Rajma as a distinct viral disease. While some viruses (e.g., certain strains of BCMV) can cause necrotic streaking or stripe-like symptoms on stems or pods, a specific "Stripe virus" is not a primary, named pathogen of Rajma. Management would fall under general mosaic control if such symptoms are observed.

5.  **Tristeza:**
    *   **Affects Rajma:** This is not known to affect Rajma. Citrus Tristeza Virus (CTV) is a significant pathogen of citrus crops and does not infect Rajma.

6.  **Stunt Virus:**
    *   **Affects Rajma:** Stunting is a common symptom caused by various viral infections in Rajma (e.g., BCMV, BYMV, CMV, Bean Pod Mottle Virus). It indicates a severe systemic infection.
    *   **Control Measures:**
        *   Since stunting is a symptom rather than a specific virus, the control measures are the same as for other viral diseases affecting Rajma, focusing on:
            *   Using resistant varieties.
            *   Planting virus-free seeds.
            *   Effective control of insect vectors (aphids, whiteflies, etc.) responsible for transmitting the specific virus causing stunting.
            *   Prompt roguing of stunted, infected plants.
            *   Maintaining good field hygiene.

**Important Note on Chemical Application:**
Always use the recommended quantity of water (typically 200-400 litres per acre for foliar sprays) to ensure thorough coverage. Rotate insecticides with different modes of action to prevent the development of resistance in insect vectors. Always read and follow the instructions on the pesticide label carefully.
