As a Senior Agronomist at Haryana Agricultural University (HAU, Hisar), I can provide the following scientifically verified advice for controlling Scale insects and Mealybugs in Almond orchards in Haryana. Both pests are known to affect Almond trees.

**General Management Principles for Scale Insects and Mealybugs:**

1.  **Regular Monitoring:** Inspect your almond trees regularly, especially the bark, twigs, branches, and undersides of leaves, for the presence of these pests. Early detection is crucial for effective control.
2.  **Pruning:** During the dormant season, prune and destroy heavily infested branches and twigs. This reduces the pest population and improves spray penetration.
3.  **Orchard Hygiene:** Keep the orchard free of weeds and debris, which can harbor pests and provide alternative hosts.
4.  **Biological Control:** Encourage natural enemies such as ladybird beetles, lacewings, and parasitic wasps, which can help in suppressing pest populations. Avoid broad-spectrum insecticides that harm these beneficial insects.

---

**Specific Control Measures for Scale Insects:**

Scale insects are often protected by a waxy or hard covering, making them difficult to control. Timing of application is critical, especially targeting the vulnerable crawler stage.

*   **Dormant Season Spray (Late Winter/Early Spring before bud break):**
    *   Apply **Horticultural Mineral Oil (HMO)** at a concentration of 20-30 ml/L of water. This suffocates overwintering scales and eggs. Ensure thorough coverage of the bark and branches.

*   **Growing Season Spray (When crawlers are active, typically post-bloom):**
    *   **Buprofezin** 25% SC: 1.25 ml/L of water. This is an insect growth regulator effective against nymphs (crawlers).
    *   **Spirotetramat** 150 OD: 0.75 ml/L of water. This is a systemic insecticide that moves both upwards and downwards in the plant, providing good control.
    *   **Imidacloprid** 17.8% SL: 0.6 ml/L of water. A systemic insecticide effective against sucking pests.
    *   **Thiamethoxam** 25% WG: 0.2 g/L of water. Another systemic insecticide that provides good control.

---

**Specific Control Measures for Mealybugs:**

Mealybugs are characterized by their white, waxy, cottony appearance and are often found in clusters.

*   **When infestations are observed (during the growing season):**
    *   **Horticultural Mineral Oil (HMO):** 10 ml/L of water. Can be used alone or in combination with other insecticides (check compatibility) to enhance penetration through the waxy coating.
    *   **Buprofezin** 25% SC: 1.25 ml/L of water. Effective against mealybug nymphs.
    *   **Spirotetramat** 150 OD: 0.75 ml/L of water. Provides systemic control against mealybugs.
    *   **Imidacloprid** 17.8% SL: 0.6 ml/L of water. A widely used systemic insecticide for mealybugs.
    *   **Acetamiprid** 20% SP: 0.2 g/L of water. Another systemic insecticide effective against mealybugs.
    *   **Thiamethoxam** 25% WG: 0.2 g/L of water. Effective systemic control.

---

**Important Considerations:**

*   **Thorough Coverage:** Ensure complete and thorough spray coverage, especially targeting the undersides of leaves, bark crevices, and areas where pests are clustered.
*   **Rotation of Chemicals:** To prevent the development of pesticide resistance, rotate insecticides with different modes of action.
*   **Pre-Harvest Interval (PHI):** Always strictly adhere to the Pre-Harvest Interval (PHI) mentioned on the product label to ensure the safety of the harvested produce.
*   **Safety Precautions:** Always wear appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) such as gloves, masks, and protective clothing while handling and applying pesticides. Follow all instructions and safety guidelines on the product label.
*   **Consult Local Experts:** For precise timing of applications and specific product availability, it is always advisable to consult your local agricultural extension officer or HAU experts.
