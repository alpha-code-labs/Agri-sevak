### Suitable Climate for Sorghum (Jowar)

Sorghum is a warm-season crop well-suited to the semi-arid and arid regions of Haryana.

*   **Temperature:**

    *   Sorghum is sensitive to frost, especially during flowering and grain filling stages.
*   **Rainfall:**
    *   It is a drought-tolerant crop and can thrive with moderate rainfall, typically requiring 400-600 mm of well-distributed rainfall during its growing season.
    *   It can also perform reasonably well in areas receiving as low as 300 mm rainfall, making it a reliable crop for rainfed areas of Haryana.
*   **Sunshine:** Requires abundant sunshine for optimal growth and yield.

### Suitable Soil Type for Sorghum (Jowar)

Sorghum is adaptable to a wide range of soil types, but performs best under specific conditions.

*   **Texture:**
    *   Prefers well-drained, fertile loamy to sandy loam soils.
    *   Can also be grown successfully on heavier soils like clay loams, provided they have good drainage.
    *   Avoid very light, sandy soils with poor water retention capacity unless irrigation is readily available.
*   **pH:**
    *   Optimal soil pH range is between 6.0 and 7.5.
    *   It exhibits good tolerance to slightly acidic (down to pH 5.0) and moderately alkaline (up to pH 8.5) conditions, making it suitable for some problematic soils in Haryana.
*   **Fertility:** Responds well to fertile soils with good organic matter content.

### Drainage Requirement for Sorghum (Jowar)

Proper drainage is crucial for healthy Sorghum growth and yield.

*   **Waterlogging Sensitivity:** Sorghum is highly sensitive to waterlogging, especially during its early growth stages. Stagnant water can lead to root rot, nutrient deficiencies, and significant yield losses.
*   **Requirement:** Fields must have excellent internal and surface drainage to prevent water accumulation.
*   **Field Preparation:** Proper land leveling and the creation of drainage channels are recommended, particularly in areas prone to heavy rainfall or where irrigation is applied.
*   **Consequences of Poor Drainage:** Poor drainage can severely stunt plant growth, reduce tillering, and ultimately lead to poor grain development.
