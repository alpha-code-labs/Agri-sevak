**Suitable Climate for Lentil (Masur):**
Lentil is a cool-season crop that thrives in a cool and dry climate.

*   **Temperature:**
    *   Requires cool temperatures during its vegetative growth and reproductive stages.
    *   Optimum temperature range for growth is generally between 18째C to 30째C.
    *   Temperatures below 5째C or above 32째C can adversely affect growth and yield.
    *   High temperatures, especially during flowering and pod development, can lead to flower drop and poor pod setting.
*   **Frost:**
    *   Highly susceptible to frost, particularly at the flowering and pod formation stages, which can cause significant yield losses. A frost-free period is crucial during these critical stages.
*   **Rainfall:**
    *   Requires minimal rainfall during its growth period, typically grown on residual soil moisture or with limited irrigation in Haryana. Excessive rainfall, especially during flowering, can lead to disease incidence and poor pod setting.

**Suitable Soil Type for Lentil (Masur):**
Lentil can adapt to a range of soil types but performs best in specific conditions.

*   **Texture:**
    *   Well-drained loamy to sandy loam soils are ideal.
    *   Can also be grown successfully on light alluvial soils.
    *   Heavy clay soils, especially those prone to waterlogging, should be avoided as they hinder root development and nitrogen fixation.
*   **pH:**
    *   Prefers a soil pH range of 6.0 to 8.0, with optimum growth observed between 6.0 and 7.5.
    *   It has a moderate tolerance to slightly alkaline conditions compared to some other legumes.
*   **Fertility:**
    *   Generally requires soils of medium fertility. Being a legume, it fixes atmospheric nitrogen, reducing the need for high nitrogen fertilization.

**Drainage Requirement for Lentil (Masur):**
Lentil is highly sensitive to waterlogging.

*   **Excellent Drainage:**
    *   Requires excellent soil drainage. Stagnant water, even for short periods, is detrimental to the crop.
    *   Poor drainage leads to root rot, impaired nitrogen fixation, and overall poor plant health, resulting in significant yield reduction or crop failure.
*   **Aeration:**
    *   Well-aerated soils are crucial for healthy root development and the efficient functioning of nitrogen-fixing nodules.
