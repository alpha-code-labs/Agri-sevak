Both these pests are sap-sucking insects that can significantly reduce plant vigour and yield if not managed effectively.

Here are the recommended control measures:

**For Scale Insects and Mealybugs:**

These pests often occur together and can be managed with similar approaches. Early detection and thorough application are key.

*   **Cultural Control:**
    *   Regularly inspect plants for early signs of infestation.
    *   Remove and destroy heavily infested plant parts to prevent spread.
    *   Maintain good field hygiene by removing weeds and plant debris.
    *   Avoid excessive nitrogen fertilization, which can promote succulent growth favoured by these pests.

*   **Chemical Control (Foliar Spray):**
    For effective control, ensure thorough coverage of the plant, including the undersides of leaves and stem junctions where these pests tend to aggregate. Repeat applications may be necessary based on pest pressure and product label recommendations.

    *   **Systemic Insecticides:** These are effective as they are absorbed by the plant and move through its tissues, killing pests that feed on the sap.
        *   **Imidacloprid 17.8% SL:** Apply at a rate of 0.5 - 1.0 ml per litre of water.
        *   **Thiamethoxam 25% WG:** Apply at a rate of 0.2 - 0.4 g per litre of water.
        *   **Spirotetramat 150 OD:** Apply at a rate of 0.75 - 1.0 ml per litre of water. This is particularly effective against sucking pests like mealybugs and scales.

    *   **Insect Growth Regulators (IGR):** These chemicals interfere with the insect's growth and development, primarily targeting nymphs.
        *   **Buprofezin 25% SC:** Apply at a rate of 1.25 - 1.5 ml per litre of water. This is effective against nymphs and helps in long-term control.

    *   **Contact Insecticides (for immediate knockdown):**
        *   **Malathion 50% EC:** Apply at a rate of 1.0 - 2.0 ml per litre of water. Ensure direct contact with the pests for best results.

    *   **Horticultural Oils:** These work by suffocating the insects and their eggs.
        *   **Paraffinic Oil (Horticultural Mineral Oil):** Apply at a rate of 10 - 20 ml per litre of water. Ensure complete coverage. Avoid spraying during very hot conditions to prevent phytotoxicity.

Always follow the instructions on the product label regarding dosage, application method, safety precautions, and pre-harvest interval (PHI), especially for a medicinal crop like Safed Musli. It is advisable to rotate different classes of insecticides to prevent the development of resistance.
