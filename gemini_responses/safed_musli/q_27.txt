As a Senior Agronomist at Haryana Agricultural University (HAU, Hisar), I can provide the following scientifically verified information regarding the suitable climate, soil type, and drainage requirements for Safed Musli (Chlorophytum borivilianum) cultivation in Haryana:

**1. Climate Requirements:**
Safed Musli thrives in a tropical to sub-tropical climate.

*   **Temperature:** It prefers a warm and humid climate. The ideal temperature range for its growth is generally between 20째C to 35째C. Temperatures below 10째C or above 40째C can negatively impact growth and tuber development.
*   **Rainfall:** It requires moderate rainfall, typically 1000-1500 mm annually, well-distributed during the growing season (monsoon months). Excessive rainfall leading to waterlogging is detrimental.
*   **Humidity:** High humidity (70-80%) during the growing phase is beneficial.
*   **Sunlight:** Safed Musli prefers partial shade or diffused sunlight. Direct, intense sunlight, especially during peak summer, can cause scorching of leaves. In open field conditions, intercropping with taller plants or using shade nets (25-30% shade) can be beneficial.

**2. Soil Type Requirements:**
The quality of soil is crucial for the healthy development of Safed Musli tubers.

*   **Texture:** Well-drained, light-textured soils are ideal. Sandy loam to loamy soils rich in organic matter are highly preferred. Heavy clay soils should be avoided as they impede tuber development and drainage.
*   **pH:** A slightly acidic to neutral soil pH range of 6.0 to 7.5 is considered optimal for its growth.
*   **Organic Matter:** Soils rich in organic matter (at least 1.5-2%) promote better tuber formation and yield. Incorporating well-decomposed farmyard manure (FYM) or compost is highly recommended.

**3. Drainage Requirements:**
Excellent drainage is paramount for Safed Musli cultivation to prevent tuber rot.

*   **Waterlogging:** Safed Musli is highly susceptible to waterlogging, which can lead to fungal diseases and complete crop failure due to tuber rot.
*   **Internal Drainage:** The soil must have good internal drainage to allow excess water to percolate quickly.
*   **Field Preparation:** Preparing raised beds (15-30 cm high) is a highly recommended practice, especially in areas prone to heavy rainfall or with less permeable subsoil. This ensures proper aeration around the tubers and prevents water accumulation.
*   **Slope:** A gentle slope in the field can also aid in surface drainage.
