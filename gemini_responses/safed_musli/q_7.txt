As a Senior Agronomist at Haryana Agricultural University (HAU, Hisar), I can confirm the following regarding the diseases mentioned for Safed Musli:

*   **Dieback:** This is not known to affect Safed Musli.
*   **Twig blight:** This is not known to affect Safed Musli.
*   **Tikka disease:** This is not known to affect Safed Musli.

Safed Musli (Chlorophytum borivilianum) is an herbaceous plant and does not typically suffer from diseases described as "dieback" or "twig blight," which are more common in woody plants. "Tikka disease" is primarily associated with groundnut (peanut) and is not a known pathogen of Safed Musli.

For general plant health and common fungal issues like leaf spots or blights that *can* affect Safed Musli, good cultural practices are crucial:

*   Ensure proper drainage to prevent waterlogging.
*   Maintain appropriate plant spacing for good air circulation.
*   Use disease-free planting material.
*   Practice field sanitation by removing and destroying infected plant debris.
