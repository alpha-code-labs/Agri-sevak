As a Senior Agronomist at Haryana Agricultural University (HAU, Hisar), I can confirm the following regarding the conditions you've inquired about for Safed Musli (*Chlorophytum borivilianum*):

*   **Scab:** This is not known to affect Safed Musli.
*   **Canker:** This is not known to affect Safed Musli.
*   **Gummosis:** This is not known to affect Safed Musli.
*   **Lichen:** This is not known to affect Safed Musli.

Safed Musli is a herbaceous plant, and the conditions mentioned (Scab, Canker, Gummosis, Lichen) are typically associated with woody plants, specific types of fungal/bacterial infections not common to Safed Musli, or non-parasitic growths. Common diseases in Safed Musli include leaf spot, leaf blight, and root rot, which require different management strategies.
