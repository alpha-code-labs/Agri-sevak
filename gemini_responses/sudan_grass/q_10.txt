As a Senior Agronomist at Haryana Agricultural University (HAU, Hisar), I provide the following scientifically verified advice for controlling pests in Sudan Grass in Haryana:

Effective pest management in Sudan Grass (Sorghum Ã— drummondii) is crucial for ensuring good fodder yield and quality. Always prioritize Integrated Pest Management (IPM) strategies, combining cultural, biological, and chemical methods.

### 1. Borers (e.g., Spotted Stem Borer - *Chilo partellus*)

**Symptoms:**
*   Initial damage appears as pinholes or shot-holes on young leaves.
*   Later, the central shoot dries up, leading to a characteristic "dead heart" symptom.
*   Boreholes and frass (excreta) may be visible on the stems.

**Control Measures:**
*   **Cultural:**
    *   Practice early sowing to avoid peak pest incidence.
    *   Remove and destroy crop stubble after harvest to eliminate overwintering larvae.
    *   Monitor fields regularly, especially during the early growth stages (10-30 days after emergence).
*   **Chemical (for severe infestations, especially at early growth stages):**
    *   **Chlorantraniliprole 18.5% SC:** Apply 60 ml per acre, mixed in 200-300 litres of water and sprayed, ensuring good coverage of the plant whorl.
    *   **Emamectin benzoate 5% SG:** Apply 80-100 g per acre, mixed in 200-300 litres of water and sprayed, targeting the plant whorl.
    *   *Note:* For fodder crops, strictly adhere to the Pre-Harvest Interval (PHI) mentioned on the pesticide label to ensure fodder safety.

### 2. Caterpillars (General defoliators, e.g., Fall Armyworm - *Spodoptera frugiperda*, Armyworms)

**Symptoms:**
*   Irregular holes or ragged feeding on leaves.
*   Presence of larvae (caterpillars) in the leaf whorls or on the underside of leaves.
*   Heavy feeding can lead to skeletonized leaves or complete defoliation in severe cases.

**Control Measures:**
*   **Cultural:**
    *   Regular scouting of fields to detect infestations early.
    *   Hand-picking and destruction of larvae in small, localized infestations.
    *   Encourage natural enemies like parasitic wasps, predatory birds, and ground beetles.
*   **Chemical (for moderate to severe infestations):**
    *   **Chlorantraniliprole 18.5% SC:** Apply 60 ml per acre, mixed in 200-300 litres of water and sprayed.
    *   **Emamectin benzoate 5% SG:** Apply 80-100 g per acre, mixed in 200-300 litres of water and sprayed.
    *   **Flubendiamide 20% WG:** Apply 50 g per acre, mixed in 200-300 litres of water and sprayed.
    *   **Lambda-cyhalothrin 5% EC:** Apply 200 ml per acre, mixed in 200-300 litres of water and sprayed (use with caution, observe PHI).
    *   *Note:* Ensure thorough spray coverage, especially into the leaf whorls where caterpillars often hide.

### 3. Diamondback Moth (*Plutella xylostella*)

*   This is not known to affect Sudan Grass. Diamondback moth is a specific pest of cruciferous crops (e.g., cabbage, cauliflower, mustard, radish).

### 4. Leaf folder (e.g., *Cnaphalocrocis medinalis* or similar species)

**Symptoms:**
*   Leaves are folded longitudinally and tied together with silken threads.
*   Larvae feed from within the folded leaves, scraping the green matter and leaving white streaks.
*   In severe cases, multiple leaves may be folded together.

**Control Measures:**
*   **Cultural:**
    *   Maintain field sanitation by removing weeds and alternate host plants.
    *   Avoid excessive nitrogen fertilization, which can promote lush vegetative growth favorable for leaf folders.
*   **Chemical (for severe infestations):**
    *   **Chlorantraniliprole 18.5% SC:** Apply 60 ml per acre, mixed in 200-300 litres of water and sprayed.
    *   **Emamectin benzoate 5% SG:** Apply 80-100 g per acre, mixed in 200-300 litres of water and sprayed.
    *   **Lambda-cyhalothrin 5% EC:** Apply 200 ml per acre, mixed in 200-300 litres of water and sprayed.

**Important Considerations for Fodder Crops:**
*   Always read and strictly follow the instructions, dosage, and Pre-Harvest Interval (PHI) mentioned on the pesticide label.
*   Ensure that the chosen chemical is approved for use on fodder crops and adheres to residue limits.
*   For the most current and specific recommendations for Sudan Grass in Haryana, always refer to the latest "Package of Practices for Kharif Crops" published by Haryana Agricultural University (HAU).
