*   **Climate:**
    *   Celery is a cool-season crop that thrives in moderate temperatures.
    *   It prefers a long, cool growing season without extreme heat or severe frost.

*   **Soil Type:**
    *   Celery performs best in highly fertile, deep, and well-drained soils.
    *   Loamy soils, particularly sandy loams or silt loams, rich in organic matter, are ideal.
    *   The soil should have excellent water-holding capacity while allowing for good aeration.
    *   A soil pH range of 6.0 to 7.0 is generally preferred for optimal nutrient availability.

*   **Drainage Requirement:**
    *   Good drainage is crucial for celery cultivation to prevent waterlogging, which can lead to root rot and other fungal diseases.
    *   While celery requires consistent moisture, it cannot tolerate standing water.
    *   The soil should be well-drained but also capable of retaining adequate moisture, often achieved by incorporating a significant amount of organic matter.
