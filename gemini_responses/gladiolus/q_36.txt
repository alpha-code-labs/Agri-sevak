First, it is important to clarify that Gladiolus is primarily grown for its ornamental flowers, not for fruit. Therefore, we will address "Flower Quality" instead of "Fruit Quality."

Here's how you can improve yield, flower quality, and storage life for Gladiolus:

### 1. Improving Yield (Flower and Corm Production)

To maximize the number and quality of flower spikes and ensure good corm/cormel production:

*   **Variety Selection:** Choose high-yielding, disease-resistant varieties well-suited to Haryana's agro-climatic conditions. Consult HAU's recommendations for specific varieties.
*   **Corm Quality:** Plant healthy, disease-free corms of optimal size (4-6 cm diameter). Smaller corms may produce fewer or inferior spikes.
*   **Optimal Planting Time:** Plant corms at the recommended time for your region in Haryana, typically from September to November for winter flowering, or February to March for summer flowering, depending on the variety and desired harvest period.
*   **Proper Spacing:** Maintain adequate spacing between corms (e.g., 20-30 cm within rows and 30-45 cm between rows) to ensure proper air circulation, light penetration, and nutrient uptake.
*   **Nutrient Management:**
    *   **Basal Application:** Incorporate well-decomposed farmyard manure (FYM) at 20-25 tonnes/acre along with a balanced dose of NPK fertilizers before planting. A general recommendation is 80-120 kg Nitrogen, 60-80 kg Phosphorus (P2O5), and 80-120 kg Potassium (K2O) per acre. Apply full Phosphorus and Potassium, and half Nitrogen as basal.
    *   **Top Dressing:** Apply the remaining half of Nitrogen in two equal splits, one at the 4-5 leaf stage and the other at spike emergence.
    *   **Micronutrients:** Foliar application of a commercial micronutrient mixture (containing Boron, Zinc, Iron, Manganese) at 2-3 g/L water during vegetative growth and pre-flowering stages can significantly improve plant health and yield.
*   **Water Management:** Provide consistent and adequate irrigation, especially during critical growth stages like spike emergence and flowering. Avoid waterlogging. Drip irrigation is highly recommended for efficient water use.
*   **Weed Control:** Keep the field free of weeds through manual weeding or appropriate herbicides to reduce competition for nutrients and water.
*   **Pest and Disease Management:** Implement integrated pest and disease management strategies to protect plants from damage, which directly impacts yield.

### 2. Improving Flower Quality (Addressing "Fruit Quality")

Gladiolus does not produce fruit. To enhance "Flower Quality" (spike length, floret size, color intensity, straightness, and overall appeal):

*   **Nutrient Management:**
    *   **Potassium (K):** Crucial for strong stems, vibrant flower color, and disease resistance. Ensure adequate K supply as mentioned above.
    *   **Boron (B):** Essential for pollen viability, flower development, and preventing bent spikes. Foliar spray of Boron (e.g., Borax 0.1% or 1 g/L water) can be beneficial if deficiency is suspected.
*   **Consistent Water Supply:** Irregular watering can lead to bent spikes, smaller florets, and premature wilting. Maintain consistent soil moisture.
*   **Pest Management:**
    *   **Thrips:** These are major pests that cause silvering of leaves and distortion/browning of florets, severely impacting flower quality.
        *   Spray with **Imidacloprid** at 0.3-0.5 ml/L water or **Thiamethoxam** at 0.2-0.4 g/L water at the first sign of infestation, repeating every 7-10 days if necessary.
*   **Disease Management:**
    *   **Fungal Diseases (e.g., Fusarium Wilt, Botrytis Blight):** Can cause wilting, corm rot, and spotting on flowers.
        *   For corm treatment before planting, dip corms in a solution of **Carbendazim** at 1-2 g/L water or **Mancozeb** at 2-3 g/L water for 30 minutes.
        *   For foliar diseases, spray **Carbendazim** at 1 g/L water or **Mancozeb** at 2 g/L water as a preventive measure or at disease onset.
*   **Staking:** For tall varieties, provide support (staking) to prevent lodging and ensure straight, upright flower spikes.

### 3. Improving Storage Life

This refers to both the post-harvest vase life of cut flowers and the storage life of corms.

**A. Post-Harvest Vase Life (Cut Flowers):**

*   **Optimal Harvesting Stage:** Harvest spikes when 1-2 florets show color, preferably in the early morning or late afternoon when temperatures are cooler.
*   **Clean Cut:** Use a sharp knife to make a clean, slanting cut at the base of the spike, leaving at least 4-5 leaves on the plant to ensure proper corm development for the next season.
*   **Immediate Hydration:** Place cut spikes immediately into clean water or a commercial floral preservative solution to prevent wilting.
*   **Pre-cooling:** Rapidly cool the harvested spikes to 4-7째C to remove field heat and slow down respiration.
*   **Holding Solutions:** Use floral preservative solutions containing sugars (for nutrition), acidifiers (to improve water uptake), and germicides (to prevent microbial growth in water).
*   **Storage Temperature:** Store cut spikes at 4-7째C with high relative humidity (90-95%) in a well-ventilated cold storage facility.
*   **Ethylene Management:** Avoid storing gladiolus near ripening fruits or other sources of ethylene gas, which can accelerate senescence (aging) of flowers.

**B. Corm Storage Life:**

*   **Corm Lifting:** Lift corms 6-8 weeks after flowering or when the leaves start to turn yellow.
*   **Curing:** After lifting, cure the corms in a warm (25-30째C), well-ventilated area with moderate humidity for 2-3 weeks. This helps to dry the corms, heal wounds, and mature the skin.
*   **Cleaning:** After curing, remove the old mother corm, roots, and any adhering soil. Separate cormels.
*   **Fungicide Treatment:** To prevent storage rots, dip corms in a solution of **Carbendazim** at 1-2 g/L water or **Mancozeb** at 2-3 g/L water for 30 minutes, then air dry thoroughly.
*   **Storage Conditions:** Store cleaned and treated corms in a cool (5-10째C), dry, well-ventilated place with moderate humidity (60-70%). Use mesh bags or shallow trays for storage to ensure air circulation.
*   **Pest Control:** Protect stored corms from pests like gladiolus thrips by dusting with an appropriate insecticide powder if necessary, or by fumigation in sealed containers.

By diligently following these scientific recommendations, you can significantly enhance the yield, flower quality, and storage life of your Gladiolus crop in Haryana. For specific local recommendations, always consult the experts at HAU, Hisar.
