As a Senior Agronomist from Haryana Agricultural University (HAU, Hisar), I will provide you with scientifically verified advice regarding the control of viral diseases in Pearl Millet (Bajra).

First, let's address the specific conditions you've mentioned:

*   **Tristeza:** This is not known to affect Pearl Millet (Bajra). Citrus Tristeza Virus (CTV) is specific to citrus crops.

For the other conditions – Leaf curl, Mosaic, Yellow vein clearing, Stripe, and Stunt virus – these are indeed symptoms or diseases that can affect Pearl Millet (Bajra). It is important to understand that there are no direct chemical treatments (viricides) available to cure a plant once it is infected with a virus. The primary strategy for managing viral diseases in Pearl Millet focuses on preventing infection by controlling the insect vectors that transmit these viruses and by adopting sound cultural practices.

Here's how to control viral diseases in Pearl Millet (Bajra):

**I. Integrated Disease Management (IDM) Strategies:**

1.  **Resistant Varieties:**
    *   The most effective and sustainable method is to cultivate Pearl Millet varieties that are resistant or tolerant to common viral diseases prevalent in Haryana. Consult with HAU or your local agricultural extension office for recommended varieties suitable for your region.

2.  **Vector Control (Insect Management):**
    *   Most viral diseases in Pearl Millet are transmitted by insect vectors such as aphids, leafhoppers, and whiteflies. Controlling these vectors is crucial.
    *   **Monitoring:** Regularly scout your fields for the presence of these insect vectors.
    *   **Chemical Control (as a last resort and based on economic threshold levels):**
        *   **For Aphids and Leafhoppers:**
            *   **Imidacloprid** 17.8% SL at 100-125 ml per acre (mixed in 200-300 litres of water).
            *   **Thiamethoxam** 25% WG at 40-50 g per acre (mixed in 200-300 litres of water).
            *   **Acetamiprid** 20% SP at 80-100 g per acre (mixed in 200-300 litres of water).
        *   **For Whiteflies:**
            *   **Buprofezin** 25% SC at 320-400 ml per acre (mixed in 200-300 litres of water).
            *   **Spiromesifen** 22.9% SC at 160-200 ml per acre (mixed in 200-300 litres of water).
            *   **Acetamiprid** 20% SP at 80-100 g per acre (mixed in 200-300 litres of water).
        *   **For Thrips:**
            *   **Fipronil** 5% SC at 400-600 ml per acre (mixed in 200-300 litres of water).
            *   **Imidacloprid** 17.8% SL at 100-125 ml per acre (mixed in 200-300 litres of water).
    *   **Note:** Always follow the manufacturer's instructions and safety precautions when using any chemical. Rotate insecticides with different modes of action to prevent resistance development.

3.  **Cultural Practices:**
    *   **Timely Sowing:** Sow Pearl Millet at the recommended time for your region. Early or late sowing can sometimes expose the crop to higher vector populations.
    *   **Field Sanitation:**
        *   Remove and destroy infected plants immediately upon identification to prevent the spread of the virus to healthy plants.
        *   Control weeds in and around the field, as many weeds can act as alternate hosts for viruses and their insect vectors.
    *   **Crop Rotation:** Practice crop rotation with non-host crops to break the disease cycle.
    *   **Nutrient Management:** Ensure balanced fertilization to promote strong, healthy plant growth, which can improve tolerance to stress, including viral infections. Avoid excessive nitrogen, which can sometimes encourage vector populations.
    *   **Barrier Crops:** Planting non-host crops like sorghum or maize around the Pearl Millet field can sometimes act as a physical barrier, reducing the initial influx of vectors.

**II. Specific Considerations for Symptoms:**

*   **Leaf Curl, Mosaic, Yellow Vein Clearing, Stripe, Stunt:** These are all symptoms indicative of viral infections. The underlying viruses are often transmitted by the insect vectors mentioned above. Therefore, the control measures outlined under "Vector Control" and "Cultural Practices" are crucial for managing these symptoms.

Remember, prevention is key when dealing with viral diseases in Pearl Millet. For specific recommendations tailored to your farm, please contact your nearest Krishi Vigyan Kendra (KVK) or the Department of Agriculture, Haryana.
