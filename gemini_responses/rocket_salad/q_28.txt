As a Senior Agronomist at HAU, Hisar, I understand the challenges faced by our farmers in protecting their crops from adverse environmental conditions. Rocket salad (Eruca vesicaria), being a cool-season crop, is particularly sensitive to extreme temperatures and intense sunlight prevalent in Haryana. Here's how you can protect your Rocket salad crop from these environmental stresses:

### Protection from Extreme Cold and Frost:

Rocket salad can tolerate light frost, but severe cold and freezing temperatures can cause significant damage, leading to wilting, discoloration, and reduced yield.

*   **Row Covers/Low Tunnels:** Utilize spun-bonded non-woven fabric (agro-textile) or clear plastic sheets to create low tunnels or direct row covers over your Rocket salad beds. These materials trap heat, protect from direct frost, and reduce wind chill. Ensure proper ventilation on warmer days to prevent excessive humidity and heat buildup.
*   **Mulching:** Apply a layer of organic mulch (e.g., straw, dry leaves, sugarcane trash) around the base of the plants. This helps insulate the soil, keeping it warmer and protecting roots from freezing.
*   **Irrigation:** Lightly irrigate the crop before a predicted frost event. Moist soil retains heat better than dry soil, releasing it slowly overnight and helping to keep the air around the plants slightly warmer. Avoid overhead irrigation during freezing temperatures as it can lead to ice formation on leaves.
*   **Site Selection:** If possible, plant Rocket salad in areas naturally sheltered from cold winds, such as near a wall or hedge.

### Protection from Heat/Loo (Hot Winds) and Intense Sunlight:

High temperatures, hot dry winds (Loo), and intense sunlight can cause Rocket salad to bolt prematurely (flower and set seed), become bitter, and suffer from leaf scorch.

*   **Shade Netting:** Install green or black shade nets with 30-50% shade density over your Rocket salad beds. This significantly reduces the intensity of sunlight and lowers the ambient temperature around the plants, preventing scorching and delaying bolting.
*   **Frequent Light Irrigation:** During hot periods, maintain consistent soil moisture through frequent, light irrigation. This helps cool the soil and plants through evapotranspiration. Avoid waterlogging.
*   **Mulching:** As with cold protection, a layer of organic mulch helps keep the soil cool and moist by reducing evaporation and insulating the soil from direct sun.
*   **Windbreaks:** Plant taller crops or erect physical barriers (e.g., jute screens) on the windward side of your Rocket salad beds to protect them from hot, dry winds (Loo).
*   **Planting Time:** Adjust your planting schedule to avoid the peak summer months when temperatures are highest. For Haryana, late autumn to early spring is generally ideal for Rocket salad cultivation.

By implementing these cultural and physical protection methods, you can significantly mitigate the adverse effects of environmental stress and ensure a healthy, productive Rocket salad crop.
