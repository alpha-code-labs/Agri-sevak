As a Senior Agronomist at Haryana Agricultural University (HAU, Hisar), I can provide you with the scientifically verified requirements for successful garlic cultivation in Haryana. Understanding these fundamental aspects of climate, soil, and drainage is crucial for achieving optimal yields and quality.

Here are the suitable requirements for garlic:

*   **Climate:**
    *   Garlic is a cool-season crop, ideally suited for the Rabi season in Haryana.
    *   **Vegetative Growth:** Requires cool temperatures ranging from 12°C to 24°C during its initial growth phase. This period typically occurs from November to January in Haryana.
    *   **Bulb Development:** Warmer temperatures, generally between 25°C and 30°C, coupled with longer day lengths (10-14 hours), are essential for proper bulb formation and maturity. This usually coincides with February and March in our region.
    *   **Vernalization:** A period of cool temperatures (below 20°C) for 1-2 months is necessary for proper bulbing. Without adequate chilling, plants may remain vegetative or produce small, poorly formed bulbs.
    *   **Frost Tolerance:** Young garlic plants can tolerate mild frost, but severe frost can cause damage, especially to developing leaves.
    *   **Humidity:** Moderate humidity is preferred; very high humidity can increase the risk of fungal diseases.

*   **Soil Type:**
    *   **Texture:** Garlic thrives best in well-drained, friable, sandy loam to loamy soils. These soils allow for good root penetration and bulb expansion. Heavy clay soils are generally unsuitable due to their poor drainage and tendency to compact, which can hinder bulb development and lead to misshapen bulbs.
    *   **pH:** A slightly acidic to neutral soil pH range of 6.0 to 7.5 is considered optimal for nutrient availability and garlic growth.
    *   **Organic Matter:** Soils rich in organic matter are highly beneficial as they improve soil structure, water retention capacity, and nutrient supply, leading to healthier plants and better yields.
    *   **Salinity:** Garlic is sensitive to saline and alkaline soils, which can negatively impact growth and yield.

*   **Drainage Requirement:**
    *   **Crucial:** Excellent drainage is paramount for garlic cultivation. Garlic is highly susceptible to waterlogging at all stages of growth.
    *   **Impact of Poor Drainage:** Waterlogged conditions deprive roots of oxygen, leading to root rot, stunted growth, poor nutrient uptake, and significantly reduced bulb size and quality. It also increases the incidence of soil-borne diseases.
    *   **Recommendation:** Ensure that the chosen field has good internal drainage. In areas prone to water accumulation, cultivation on raised beds (ridges) is highly recommended to prevent waterlogging around the root zone. This practice is particularly important during periods of heavy rainfall or excessive irrigation.

By carefully considering these factors, you can establish a strong foundation for a successful garlic crop in Haryana.
