### Control of Physical Damage in Papaya

**1. Fruit Bursting (Fruit Cracking/Splitting)**

This condition is primarily caused by sudden fluctuations in soil moisture (e.g., a dry spell followed by heavy rain or irrigation), high humidity, rapid fruit growth, and deficiencies of certain micronutrients.

*   **Irrigation Management:** Maintain consistent soil moisture throughout the fruit development period. Avoid extremes of drought followed by sudden heavy irrigation. Drip irrigation is highly recommended for precise water management.
*   **Nutrient Management:** Ensure adequate supply of Boron and Calcium, which are crucial for cell wall integrity and fruit development.
    *   **Foliar application of Boron:** Apply **Disodium octaborate tetrahydrate** (20% Boron) at 1-1.5 g/L of water, starting from fruit set and repeating every 3-4 weeks, especially during periods of rapid fruit growth.
    *   **Foliar application of Calcium:** Apply **Calcium nitrate** at 3-5 g/L of water, starting from fruit set and repeating every 3-4 weeks.
*   **Shade Management:** Provide partial shade to developing fruits during periods of intense sunlight, especially in hot summer months, to reduce stress on the fruit skin.
*   **Variety Selection:** Choose papaya varieties known to be less susceptible to fruit cracking.

**2. Leaf Burning/Melting**

Leaf burning (sunscald) is typically caused by intense direct sunlight, heat stress, or improper chemical application. "Melting" can occur under severe heat and humidity stress, or conditions leading to waterlogging and root damage.

*   **Shade Management:** Provide partial shade, especially during the peak summer months (April-June) in Haryana, to protect plants from intense solar radiation. This can be achieved using shade nets (30-50% shade).
*   **Irrigation:** Ensure adequate and consistent water supply, especially during hot and dry periods, to prevent water stress. Avoid overhead irrigation during peak sun hours, as water droplets can magnify sunlight and cause scorching.
*   **Proper Chemical Application:**
    *   Always follow recommended dosage rates for all pesticides and fertilizers. Over-application can lead to phytotoxicity.
    *   Avoid spraying during the hottest part of the day or when temperatures are excessively high, as this increases the risk of chemical burn.
    *   Ensure proper calibration of spray equipment to ensure uniform and correct application.
*   **Soil Drainage:** Ensure good soil drainage to prevent waterlogging, which can lead to root damage and subsequent leaf symptoms resembling "melting" due to severe plant stress.

**3. Hardness in Fruit (Failure to Ripen Properly)**

If papaya fruit remains hard and fails to soften or ripen properly, it is usually due to premature harvesting, suboptimal post-harvest conditions, or environmental factors affecting the ripening process.

*   **Harvesting at Correct Maturity:** Harvest fruits when they show the first signs of yellowing or color break (typically 1/4th to 1/3rd yellowing). Fruits picked too green will not ripen well or will have poor flavor and texture.
*   **Optimal Ripening Conditions (Post-harvest):**
    *   Maintain appropriate temperature (20-25Â°C) and humidity (85-90%) for ripening.
    *   **Ethylene Application (Post-harvest):** For uniform and accelerated ripening, fruits can be treated with ethylene-releasing agents.
        *   **Ethephon** (39% SL) can be used as a post-harvest dip or spray at a concentration of 500-1000 ppm (1.3-2.6 ml/L of water) for 5-10 minutes, depending on the maturity stage and desired ripening speed. Ensure proper ventilation during the ripening process.
*   **Field Management:** Ensure overall plant health and balanced nutrition to support proper fruit development and physiological maturity, which are prerequisites for good ripening.
