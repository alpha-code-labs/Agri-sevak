Thank you for reaching out to Haryana Agricultural University for guidance. As a Senior Agronomist, I can confirm the following regarding rust diseases in Guar:

*   **Black Rust, Brown Rust, Yellow Rust, White Rust, and Red Rust:** These specific rust diseases, as commonly understood for cereals or other crops, are not known to affect Guar.

However, Guar (Cluster Bean) is susceptible to a specific rust disease caused by *Uromyces appendiculatus*. This disease manifests as reddish-brown pustules primarily on the leaves, and in severe cases, can also affect stems and pods, leading to defoliation and significant yield loss.

For the effective management of Guar Rust (*Uromyces appendiculatus*), consider the following integrated approach:

**1. Cultural Practices:**
*   **Resistant Varieties:** Where available and suitable for your region, planting rust-resistant Guar varieties is the most economical and environmentally friendly approach.
*   **Crop Rotation:** Avoid continuous cultivation of Guar in the same field. Rotate with non-host crops to break the disease cycle.
*   **Field Sanitation:** Remove and destroy infected plant debris after harvest to reduce inoculum for the next season.
*   **Timely Sowing:** Sowing at the recommended time can help plants escape peak disease periods.

**2. Chemical Control (Fungicides):**
Fungicides should be applied preventatively or at the very first appearance of symptoms. Ensure thorough coverage of the foliage. Always follow the manufacturer's instructions and safety precautions.

*   **For systemic control (effective against established infections):**
    *   **Propiconazole 25% EC:** Apply at 250-300 ml per acre in 200-300 litres of water.
    *   **Difenoconazole 25% EC:** Apply at 120-150 ml per acre in 200-300 litres of water.
    *   **Azoxystrobin 23% SC:** Apply at 200 ml per acre in 200-300 litres of water.

*   **For contact/preventative control (can be rotated with systemic fungicides):**
    *   **Mancozeb 75% WP:** Apply at 600-800 g per acre in 200-300 litres of water.

It is advisable to rotate fungicides with different modes of action to prevent the development of resistance. For the most current and specific recommendations tailored to your exact location and Guar variety, please consult your local agricultural extension officer.
