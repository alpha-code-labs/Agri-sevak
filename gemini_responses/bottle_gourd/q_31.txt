As a Senior Agronomist at Haryana Agricultural University (HAU, Hisar), I can provide the following scientifically verified advice for controlling physical damage in Bottle Gourd in Haryana:

---

### 1. Control of Fruit Bursting (Cracking)

Fruit bursting is primarily a physiological disorder caused by sudden fluctuations in soil moisture, leading to rapid water uptake and internal pressure exceeding the fruit skin's elasticity. Nutrient deficiencies, particularly Boron and Calcium, can also contribute to weaker cell walls.

*   **Maintain Consistent Soil Moisture:** The most critical step is to provide uniform and consistent irrigation. Avoid long dry spells followed by sudden heavy irrigation, which can cause rapid water uptake and fruit cracking.
*   **Mulching:** Apply organic mulch (e.g., straw, dry leaves, crop residues) around the base of the plants. Mulching helps conserve soil moisture, reduces evaporation, and moderates soil temperature, thereby preventing extreme moisture fluctuations.
*   **Balanced Nutrition:** Ensure adequate availability of Boron and Calcium, which are crucial for cell wall integrity and fruit elasticity.
    *   For Boron deficiency:
        *   Foliar spray of **Disodium octaborate tetrahydrate** at 1-1.5 g/L of water during flowering and fruit development stages.
        *   Soil application of **Sodium tetraborate decahydrate** (Borax) at 10-15 kg/acre before planting or as a side dressing.
    *   For Calcium deficiency:
        *   Foliar spray of **Calcium chloride** at 2-3 g/L of water or **Calcium nitrate** at 3-4 g/L of water at the fruit development stage.

### 2. Control of Leaf Burning/Melting

Leaf burning or melting is typically a result of environmental stress, including high temperatures, intense sunlight, water stress, or sometimes poor drainage leading to root issues.

*   **Adequate and Consistent Irrigation:** Ensure plants receive sufficient water, especially during hot and dry periods. Irrigate in the early morning or late evening to minimize water loss through evaporation and prevent water stress.
*   **Shade Management:** During peak summer months (April-June) when temperatures are very high and sunlight is intense, consider using shade nets (30-50% shade percentage) over the crop. This protects the foliage from direct scorching sun and reduces leaf temperature.
*   **Proper Spacing:** Maintain optimal plant spacing to ensure good air circulation around the plants, which helps in cooling and reduces humidity build-up.
*   **Avoid Over-Fertilization:** Excessive application of nitrogen, especially during hot weather, can make plants more susceptible to heat stress and leaf burn. Follow recommended fertilizer schedules.
*   **Good Drainage:** Ensure the field has good drainage to prevent waterlogging, which can damage roots and lead to symptoms resembling leaf melting due to impaired water uptake.
*   **Foliar Spray Timing:** If foliar fertilizers or pesticides are applied, do so in the cooler parts of the day (early morning or late evening) to avoid leaf burn, especially when temperatures are high.

### 3. Control of Hardness in Fruit

Hardness in Bottle Gourd fruits is usually associated with over-maturity, water stress, or nutrient imbalances.

*   **Timely Harvesting:** Harvest fruits at their tender, immature stage. Delaying harvest allows the fruit to mature, leading to increased fiber content and hardness. Regular harvesting also encourages the plant to produce more fruits.
*   **Consistent Soil Moisture:** Maintain uniform soil moisture throughout the fruit development period. Water stress can lead to tougher, less succulent fruits.
*   **Balanced Nutrition, Especially Potassium:** Potassium plays a vital role in fruit quality, size, and texture. Ensure adequate potassium supply.
    *   Soil application of **Potassium sulphate** at 20-30 kg/acre during the fruit development stage can improve fruit quality and reduce hardness.
    *   Foliar spray of **Potassium nitrate** at 5-10 g/L of water can also be applied during fruit development.
*   **Variety Selection:** Choose Bottle Gourd varieties known for good fruit quality and tenderness, suitable for the local agro-climatic conditions of Haryana.
