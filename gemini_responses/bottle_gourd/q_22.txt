Here are the recommended cultivation practices for Bottle Gourd in Haryana:

*   **Sowing Method/Pattern:**
    *   Bottle Gourd is typically sown directly in the field.
    *   Seeds are commonly sown on ridges or in pits.
    *   For trailing varieties, sowing in pits (3-4 seeds per pit) or on one side of ridges is preferred, allowing for subsequent training on trellises, pandals, or along the ground.
    *   For bush types, sowing on flat beds or ridges is also practiced.

*   **Planting Depth:**
    *   Seeds should be sown at a depth of 2-3 cm.

*   **Spacing:**
    *   Spacing varies depending on the variety and training system:
        *   **For trailing varieties (ground trailing):** Maintain a row-to-row distance of 2.5 - 3.0 meters and plant-to-plant distance of 0.6 - 1.0 meter.
        *   **For trailing varieties (trellis/pandal system):** Maintain a row-to-row distance of 2.0 - 3.0 meters and plant-to-plant distance of 1.0 - 1.5 meters.
        *   **For bush varieties:** Maintain a row-to-row distance of 1.5 - 2.0 meters and plant-to-plant distance of 0.6 - 0.9 meters.

*   **Seed Dose:**
    *   An average seed rate of 4-5 kg per acre is recommended. This may vary slightly based on seed size and germination percentage.
