Mites, particularly Red Spider Mite (Tetranychus urticae) and Rust Mite (Aculops lycopersici, though other species can affect cucurbits like *Epitrimerus cucurbitae* or *Tetranychus cinnabarinus* for red spider mite), can cause significant damage to pumpkin crops, especially during dry and warm weather conditions. They feed on plant sap, leading to yellowing, bronzing, leaf distortion, and reduced yield.

Here's a comprehensive approach to control mites in pumpkin:

**I. Integrated Pest Management (IPM) for Mites:**

1.  **Monitoring:** Regularly inspect the underside of leaves for mites, their eggs, and webbing (for red spider mites). Early detection is crucial.
2.  **Cultural Practices:**
    *   **Weed Management:** Keep the field and surrounding areas free of weeds, as many weeds act as alternate hosts for mites.
    *   **Proper Irrigation:** Maintain adequate soil moisture. Water stress can make plants more susceptible to mite infestations. Overhead irrigation can sometimes help wash off mites, but be mindful of fungal disease risks.
    *   **Crop Rotation:** Avoid planting pumpkins or other cucurbits in the same field repeatedly.
    *   **Field Sanitation:** Remove and destroy severely infested plant parts or crop residues after harvest.
3.  **Biological Control:** Encourage natural enemies like predatory mites (e.g., *Amblyseius* species), lacewings, and ladybird beetles, which feed on pest mites. Avoid broad-spectrum insecticides that harm these beneficial insects.

**II. Chemical Control (Acaricides):**

When mite populations are high and causing economic damage, chemical control may be necessary. Always rotate acaricides with different modes of action to prevent resistance development. Ensure thorough coverage, especially on the underside of leaves where mites reside.

Here are some CIBRC approved technical chemical names and their recommended dosages for mite control in cucurbits:

*   **Spiromesifen 22.9% SC:** Apply at **0.5 ml per litre of water**. This acaricide is effective against all life stages (eggs, nymphs, and adults) and has translaminar action.
*   **Fenpyroximate 5% SC:** Apply at **0.5 ml per litre of water**. It acts by contact and stomach action, controlling various mite stages.
*   **Propargite 57% EC:** Apply at **1.5 - 2.0 ml per litre of water**. This is a contact acaricide effective against motile stages of mites.
*   **Abamectin 1.9% EC:** Apply at **0.4 - 0.5 ml per litre of water**. It has translaminar activity and is effective against motile stages.
*   **Wettable Sulphur 80% WP:** Apply at **2.0 - 3.0 grams per litre of water**. Sulphur acts as a contact and fumigant acaricide, particularly effective against rust mites and also helps control red spider mites. *   **Hexythiazox 5.45% EC:** Apply at **0.5 ml per litre of water**. This is a mite growth regulator, primarily ovicidal and larvicidal, disrupting the mite life cycle.
*   **Etoxazole 10% SC:** Apply at **0.5 ml per litre of water**. Similar to hexythiazox, it's a mite growth regulator with ovicidal and larvicidal properties.

**Important Considerations for Chemical Application:**

*   **Timing:** Apply acaricides at the first sign of infestation to prevent rapid population build-up.
*   **Coverage:** Ensure complete and thorough coverage of the plant, especially the undersides of leaves, as mites are often found there.
*   **Rotation:** Always rotate between different groups of acaricides (with different modes of action) to prevent the development of resistance in mite populations.
*   **Safety:** Always wear appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) like gloves, mask, and protective clothing while handling and spraying chemicals. Follow the instructions and safety precautions mentioned on the product label.
*   **Pre-Harvest Interval (PHI):** Observe the specified Pre-Harvest Interval (PHI) for each chemical to ensure the produce is safe for consumption.

By adopting these integrated strategies, you can effectively manage mite infestations in your pumpkin crop and ensure a healthy yield. For any further clarification, please consult your local agricultural extension officer.
