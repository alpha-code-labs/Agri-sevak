### Climate Requirements for Peach

Peach (Prunus persica) is a temperate fruit crop, but several low-chilling varieties are suitable for subtropical regions like parts of Haryana.

*   **Chilling Hours:** Peach trees require a specific number of chilling hours (hours below 7째C but above 0째C) during winter for proper bud break, flowering, and fruiting.
    *   **Low-chilling varieties:** 100-400 chilling hours (suitable for plains of Haryana).
    *   **Medium-chilling varieties:** 400-700 chilling hours (suitable for cooler parts or foothills).
*   **Temperature:**
    *   **Optimum Growing Temperature:** 20-30째C during the growing season.
    *   **Winter Dormancy:** Requires cold winters for dormancy and chilling accumulation.
    *   **Spring Frost:** Peach blossoms are highly susceptible to damage by spring frosts. Temperatures below -1째C during flowering can cause significant crop loss.
    *   **Summer Heat:** While tolerant of high summer temperatures, extreme heat combined with low humidity can affect fruit quality and cause sunburn.
*   **Sunlight:** Requires abundant sunlight (at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight daily) for good fruit development, color, and sugar content.

### Soil Type Requirements for Peach

Peach trees are sensitive to soil conditions, and selecting the right soil is crucial for their health and productivity.

*   **Texture:**
    *   **Ideal:** Deep, well-drained sandy loam to loamy soils.
    *   **Acceptable:** Can tolerate a range from sandy to medium clay loams, provided drainage is excellent.
    *   **Avoid:** Heavy clay soils, as they impede drainage and aeration, leading to root problems.
*   **pH:**
    *   **Optimum:** Slightly acidic to neutral, with a pH range of 6.0 to 7.0.
    *   **Tolerance:** Can tolerate a pH range of 5.5 to 7.5, but nutrient availability can be affected outside the optimum range.
*   **Organic Matter:** Soils rich in organic matter are preferred as they improve soil structure, water retention, and nutrient availability.
*   **Salinity:** Peach trees are moderately sensitive to soil salinity. High salt concentrations can inhibit growth and reduce yield.

### Drainage Requirements for Peach

Excellent drainage is paramount for successful peach cultivation.

*   **Waterlogging:** Peach roots are highly susceptible to damage from waterlogging. Even short periods of standing water (24-48 hours) can cause root rot, nutrient deficiencies, and eventually lead to tree decline and death.
*   **Aeration:** Good drainage ensures adequate soil aeration, which is essential for healthy root respiration and nutrient uptake.
*   **Water Table:** A deep water table (preferably below 1.5-2 meters) is ideal. High water tables can lead to chronic waterlogging issues.
*   **Mitigation:** In areas with less than ideal drainage, measures like raised beds, subsoiling, or installing subsurface drainage systems (tile drains) may be necessary.
