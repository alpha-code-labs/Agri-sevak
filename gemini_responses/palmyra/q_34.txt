The harmful effects of weedicides/herbicides on Palmyra palms (Borassus flabellifer) primarily arise from improper application, leading to direct contact with the palm's foliage or root system, or from using non-selective chemicals without adequate protection. To minimize these risks and ensure the health of your Palmyra palms, consider the following scientifically verified practices:

1.  **Prioritize Non-Chemical Weed Control:**
    *   For young palms or in sensitive areas, manual weeding is the safest and most effective method.
    *   Mulching with organic materials (e.g., paddy straw, coir pith, dry leaves) around the base of the palm can effectively suppress weeds, conserve moisture, and provide a physical barrier.

2.  **Careful Herbicide Selection and Application Strategy:**
    *   **Directed Spraying:** When using herbicides, always direct the spray only onto the weeds, strictly avoiding any contact with the palm trunk, leaves, or growing point.
    *   **Non-Selective Herbicides (with extreme caution):** For general weed control around mature palms, non-selective herbicides can be used. However, they will damage any green tissue they touch.
        *   **Glyphosate** (e.g., 41% SL at 1.5-2.0 kg a.i./acre)
        *   **Paraquat dichloride** (e.g., 24% SL at 0.5-1.0 kg a.i./acre)
    *   **Pre-Emergent Herbicides (for residual control):** These are applied to the soil before weed emergence. Ensure the soil is moist for better efficacy and that the palm's root zone is not directly exposed to high concentrations. Always check product labels for specific recommendations regarding use around established trees.
        *   **Pendimethalin** (e.g., 30% EC at 1.0-1.5 kg a.i./acre)
        *   **Diuron** (e.g., 80% WP at 1.0-1.5 kg a.i./acre)

3.  **Minimize Spray Drift:**
    *   **Use Low-Pressure Sprayers:** Operate sprayers at lower pressures (e.g., 1.5-2.5 kg/cmÂ²) to produce larger, heavier droplets that are less prone to drift.
    *   **Select Drift-Reducing Nozzles:** Use flat-fan or air-induction nozzles designed to minimize fine droplets.
    *   **Spray During Calm Conditions:** Avoid spraying on windy days (wind speed exceeding 8-10 km/h). The best time is usually early morning or late evening when wind speeds are typically lower.
    *   **Use Spray Shields:** Employ physical barriers or shields around the base of the palm to prevent herbicide contact with the trunk and lower fronds.

4.  **Correct Dosage and Dilution:**
    *   **Adhere to Label Recommendations:** Always follow the manufacturer's recommended dosage rates and dilution instructions precisely. Overdosing does not necessarily improve weed control but significantly increases the risk of phytotoxicity to the palm.
    *   **Calibrate Sprayer:** Regularly calibrate your sprayer to ensure accurate application rates.

5.  **Protect the Palm's Base:**
    *   **Mulching:** Apply a thick layer of organic mulch (e.g., 10-15 cm deep) around the base of the palm, extending to the drip line. This suppresses weeds, conserves moisture, and provides a physical barrier against herbicide contact with the soil surface directly above the root zone.
    *   **Physical Barriers:** For young palms, consider wrapping the lower trunk with burlap or plastic sheeting during herbicide application in the vicinity.

6.  **Post-Application Management (in case of accidental exposure):**
    *   **Immediate Washing:** If herbicide accidentally contacts the palm's foliage, immediately wash the affected parts thoroughly with plain water to dilute and remove the chemical.
    *   **Supportive Care:** Provide adequate irrigation and balanced fertilization to help the palm recover from stress.

Always read and strictly follow the instructions on the herbicide label for safe and effective use.
